Player Reference

Your abilities: Investigative (auto-succeed, for clues) and General (roll d6, for risky actions). Both have points you spend from. Points go down as you spend; your ability level doesn't. Spend freely — you get points back during play.
Finding Things Out
Get a clue or information Have 1+ in a relevant Investigative ability → you get the info automatically. No roll. Tell the GM which ability and how you use it.
"I have Leechcraft — I examine the body." → GM tells you cause of death.
Get a bonus from an Investigative ability Spend 1 point. Describe how the ability applies. Pick one: +3 on any roll (incl. attacks) · +1d6 damage on a hit · Block 4 damage for 1 round · +3 to your defense vs 1 incoming attack · Special effect (ask GM).
Spending points doesn't reduce your expertise — you still get clues. These are examples; propose your own ideas.
Doing Risky Things (General Abilities)
Attempt something that might fail Spend points from that ability (optional) → roll 1d6 + points spent → meet or beat the target number (usually 4). Spend 3 = guaranteed success. Out of points = still roll, just the bare d6.
Get pool points back Take a Refresh token from the shared bowl. Each restores 1 spent point in any ability (not Health/Morale). Tokens vanish at end of scene — use them.
Mook dropped → 1 token. Named foe → 3. Big bad → 5. Non-combat challenge → 2-3 per player (GM call).
Combat
Know the turn order GM picks who acts first. After your turn, you pick who goes next (anyone — ally or enemy). Last person in the round picks who opens the next round.
Attack someone Choose: Warfare → damages Health (body), Sway → damages Morale (will), Sorcery → either (see your sheet).
Spend combat ability points (before rolling) → roll d6 + spent ≥ the number on the enemy's stat (usually 3–4).
Hit → roll 1d6 + damage modifier (on your sheet, from your weapon). Your d6 can't go below the points you spent (capped at 6).
Spend 4 Warfare, roll 2 = total 6. Enemy needs 4 to be hit, so you hit. Damage: d6 + damage modifier (+1). You roll 2, but spent 4, so it counts as 4. Damage = 5, minus their Armor.
Deal extra damage After a hit, spend 1 Investigative point → +1d6 damage. Describe how that ability helps.
"I spend Tactics of Death — I see the gap in his guard." → extra d6.
Hit multiple enemies Need 8+ points in your combat ability. If your attack roll exceeds what you needed by 3+, you hit one extra nearby foe (1d6+1 damage). Every further +3 = another.
Score a critical hit If your attack roll exceeds what you needed by 5+, you deal an extra 1d6 damage. Can't combine with multi-target.
Force someone to do something (or take damage) Maneuver: roll a successful attack. Your total sets how hard it is to resist. The target either does what you say (disarm, trip, flee, obey) or takes your total − 2 as damage.
Spend 3 Sway, roll 5 = total 8. "Leave this room." Guard leaves, or takes 6 Morale damage.
Help an ally's attack Teamwork: on your turn, skip attacking. Add your damage to an ally's hit, using your damage type. Lets Sway damage contribute to a Warfare hit.
Dodge a physical attack Need 8+ in Athletics. After being hit but before damage: spend Athletics points + roll. Must beat the attacker's roll (with a shield: just match). Success = attack cancelled.
Taking Damage & Healing
Know what happens when I'm hit Enemies roll vs the number on your sheet to hit you. Armor reduces physical damage; Grit reduces social/mental damage. Enemies drop at 0. Heroes go negative:
PoolHealth (Body)
0+Healthy
−1 to −5Hurt — roll d6, need 4+ or fall unconscious. Everything you try needs 1 higher to succeed.
−6 to −10Seriously Wounded — roll again. Everything needs 2 higher. Healing half as effective.
Below −10Defeated — out of fight.
PoolMorale (Will)
0+Resilient
−1 to −5Unstable — roll d6, need 4+ or you can't act. Everything needs 1 higher.
−6 to −10Panicked — roll again. Everything needs 2 higher.
Below −10Broken — surrender, flee, collapse.
Heal an ally's Morale As an action: spend 1 Sway → restore 2 Morale to an ally (or split among allies), or 1 to yourself. Trustworthy spend: 1 point → 6 Morale to ally (need at least 1 in Sway), or 3 to self.
Heal an ally's Health Bind Wounds: spend 1 point → heal 2 Health to someone else. Heal yourself: only 1 Health (2 if you have 8+ in Bind Wounds).
Other
Get a small bonus on any roll Drives: when one of your three Drives is relevant, +1 to any die result.
Have the right gear Preparedness: roll to establish you have the right item. With 8+ in Preparedness (Flashback): retroactively declare past actions — "I already bribed that guard."
Sneak past someone Stealth: roll to move unseen. With 8+ in Stealth (Vanish): disappear and reappear anywhere in the scene.
Pick a lock / steal something Burglary: roll to pick locks, steal, sleight of hand.

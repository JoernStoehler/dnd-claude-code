# Character Templates — One-Shot 2026-03-31

5 templates for 3-5 players. General abilities are pre-assigned; players choose Investigative abilities, personality, and gear.

**Build points used:** 30 General, 18 Health/Morale, 10-12 Investigative (varies by player count) + 3 Allegiance.

**Scaling Investigative points by player count:**
- 3 players: 12 Investigative + 3 Allegiance = 15 total
- 4 players: 11 Investigative + 3 Allegiance = 14 total
- 5 players: 10 Investigative + 3 Allegiance = 13 total

Each template pre-assigns 3 profession abilities (3 points) + gets 1 bonus for single-profession. Remaining points are player's choice. I've noted how many free points per player count.

---

## How to Use These Templates (Read Aloud or Hand Out)

> Welcome to Swords of the Serpentine! You're heroes in a corrupt fantasy city.
>
> **Pick a template** that sounds fun. The numbers on the left are done — don't worry about them. **Your job is to fill in the right side:**
>
> 1. **Name** your character (fantasy Italian-ish names fit the setting, but anything works)
> 2. **Adjectives** — 3-5 words that describe your personality (e.g. "cynical, stubborn, proud")
> 3. **Drives** — What three things are best in life? (e.g. "Revenge. Good wine. Loyal friends.")
> 4. **Choose your abilities** from the menu on your sheet
> 5. **Gear** — 5 items that define who you are. One is your weapon (already noted). The rest are personal — a lucky charm, a letter from home, a flask of brandy, a reputation, a debt. Gear isn't shopping; it's characterization.
>
> **You can leave ability points unspent** and assign them during play when you realize you need something!

## Template 1: THE WARRIOR

*You solve problems with steel. You're dangerous, experienced, and hard to kill. You know monsters, battlefields, and how to find the weak spot in anything.*

### Pre-Built (don't change these)

**Offense — Warfare:** Warfare 8 (Talent: Cleave — hit multiple foes if you exceed their Threshold by 3+); Damage Modifier +1 (your weapon)
**Offense — Sway:** Sway 4; Damage Modifier +1 (presence)
**Defense — Health:** Health Threshold 4, Armor 1 (worn leather or chain), Health 10
**Defense — Morale:** Morale Threshold 3, Grit 1, Morale 8

**Other General Abilities:** Athletics 5, Stealth 4, Bind Wounds 3, Preparedness 4, Burglary 2

*In a fight: Spend Warfare points before rolling to set your minimum damage. Spend 3 = auto-hit most things. At Warfare 8, if you beat the target by 3+, you Cleave and hit a second foe nearby for free.*

### Your Choices — Investigative Abilities

**Profession abilities (Warrior) — pick 2 or 3:**

| Ability | What it does | Why you'd pick it |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Know Monstrosities | You know legends about non-human creatures, their habits and weaknesses | You can identify what's hunting people and how to stop it |
| Spot Frailty | You notice structural weaknesses, armor gaps, and personality flaws | Extra combat insight + detect when something is fragile or fake |
| Tactics of Death | You can read a crime scene or battlefield and understand what happened | Reconstruct fights, understand military formations |
| Wilderness Mastery | You can track, navigate, survive outdoors, and bond with animals | Tracking creatures, finding trails, animal companions |

**Social abilities — pick 1 or 2:**

| Ability | What it does |
|---------|-------------|
| Intimidation | People find you terrifying. You can bully answers out of them. |
| Taunt | You make people angry enough to drop their guard or monologue at you. |
| Command | You give orders and people obey. Walk into a room like you belong there. |
| Trustworthy | People want to trust you. You can reassure panicking allies (restores their Morale). |

**Free points:** Spend remaining points on any ability above, or put extra ranks in abilities you already picked. (3 players: 5 free points. 4 players: 4 free. 5 players: 3 free.)

### Allegiances — pick from:

**Allies (assign 2 points — same faction or split):**
- **Mercenaries** — You know sellswords, soldiers, guards. They respect you.
- **City Watch** — You've worked with law enforcement. They owe you favors.
- **Commoners** — The working people trust you. You're one of them.
- **Outlanders** — You know travelers, barbarians, adventurers from far away.

**Enemy (assign 1 point):**
- **Thieves' Guilds** — You've crossed the underworld.
- **Sorcerous Cabals** — You've killed a sorcerer's creature. They remember.
- **Ancient Nobility** — A noble family has it out for you.
- **Monstrosities** — Something inhuman wants you dead.

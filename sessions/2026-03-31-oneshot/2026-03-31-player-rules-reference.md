# Swords of the Serpentine — Player Rules Reference

*Print one copy per player. Front and back of a single A4 sheet.*

---

> **The one rule that changes everything:** In this game, you don't roll to find clues. If you have the right ability and you're in the right place, you get the information — automatically, no roll. The mystery is what you DO with what you learn, not whether you learn it.

> **Don't hoard your points.** Spending is how you succeed. Pools refresh through tokens and rest. Unspent points at the end of an adventure are wasted opportunities.

## The Basics

You only need **one d6**. The target number is almost always **4**.

You have two kinds of abilities: **Investigative** (for finding things out) and **General** (for doing things).

### Investigative Abilities — You Get the Clue
<!-- source: rulebook.md:1094-1104 — "Investigative abilities let you find that information by giving you key (or 'core') clues if you have the right ability... You don't need to roll for these abilities, or spend any points to get them" -->

If you have **1 or more ranks** in an Investigative ability, and you're in a situation where that ability applies, **you automatically get the information.** No roll. Just tell the GM what ability you're using and how.

> *"I have Leechcraft — I examine the body."*
> *"I have Skulduggery — I look for signs of criminal activity."*
> *"I have Spirit Sight — I check for supernatural traces."*

Having a rank means you're an expert. **Spending a point doesn't make you less expert** — it just means you can do something extra cool that many times per adventure.

### Investigative Spends — Extra Cool Stuff

<!-- source: rulebook.md:3452-3464 — full list of Investigative spend options -->
Spend 1 pool point from an Investigative ability to get a powerful bonus. You can:

- Get **+3 on a related General ability test** (including attacks)
- Deal **+1d6 extra damage** on a successful attack
- Gain **Armor 4 or Grit 4** for one round
- Raise your **Threshold by +3** for one incoming attack
- Create a **special effect** (ask the GM what's possible)

Describe HOW that ability helps. *"I spend Tactics of Death — I see the gap in his armor."*

### General Abilities — Roll d6 + Spend

When you want to do something risky (fight, sneak, climb, resist), the GM tells you which General ability to use.

1. **Declare** how many pool points you want to spend (0 or more)
2. **Roll 1d6**, add the points you spent
3. Try to **meet or beat the Difficulty** (usually 4)

Spending 3 points = auto-succeed against Difficulty 4 (even rolling a 1: 1+3=4).

<!-- source: rulebook.md:1089-1091 — "Your pool of points reduces and increases as you spend and refresh it, but it can never exceed that ability's rank." -->
<!-- source: rulebook.md:2526 — "Rank 0 in a General ability means that unless you are attacking, you may not roll the die" — so pool 0 with rank 1+ is fine, you still roll. -->
Your pool goes down as you spend, but your **rank never changes.** Pools refill through Refresh tokens and rest. **When your pool hits 0, you can still roll — you just roll the bare d6 with no bonus.**

## Combat

### How to Attack

<!-- source: rulebook.md:4237-4266 — GM picks who goes first, then each actor picks the next. Last actor of the round picks who opens the next round. -->
**Turn order:** The GM picks who acts first. After you act, you choose who goes next (friend or foe). Last person in the round picks who starts the next round. No initiative roll.

1. Pick a target
2. Choose: **Warfare** (attacks Health), **Sway** (attacks Morale), or **Sorcery** (attacks Health or Morale). Both tracks defeat enemies — Health 0 = knocked out or killed, Morale 0 = surrendered, fled, or broken.
3. Spend points from that ability if you want (before rolling)
4. Roll d6 + points spent. Try to meet or beat the target's **Threshold** (usually 3 or 4)
<!-- source: rulebook.md:3334 — "Your minimum result on that damage die roll is the number of points (up to 6) you spent while attacking" -->
5. If you hit: roll **1d6 + Damage Modifier** for damage. Your minimum result on the damage die = points you spent (max 6)

### Damage Example

> You spend 4 Warfare and roll a 2. You hit (2+4=6, threshold was 4). For damage you roll 1d6+1 (weapon modifier). You roll a 2, but because you spent 4 points, your minimum damage die is 4. So damage = 4+1 = **5 Health damage** to the target, minus their Armor.

### Critical Hits and Multi-Target

<!-- source: rulebook.md:4453-4454 (crits), 4469-4477 (multi-target), 4464-4465 (choose one) -->
- **Critical Hit:** Beat the threshold by 5+ → extra 1d6 damage
- **Multi-Target (Cleave/Blast/Play to the Crowd):** If your combat ability is **8+** AND you beat the threshold by 3+ → hit an additional nearby foe. +3 more = another foe. Extra foes take base damage (1d6+1 or Fixed Damage 4).
- You choose: Critical Hit OR multi-target, not both on the same attack.

### Investigative Spends in Combat

After a successful hit, spend an Investigative pool point for **+1d6 bonus damage** if you can explain how that ability helps. This is huge — don't forget it!

> *"I spend Spot Frailty to exploit the crack in his shield — extra d6 of damage."*
> *"I spend Taunt — I mock his terrible swordsmanship so badly he flinches into my blade."*

### Maneuvers — "Do This or Take Damage"

Instead of normal damage, you can force a choice on your target:

1. Roll a successful attack (Warfare, Sway, or Sorcery)
2. Your attack result becomes the **Difficulty**
<!-- source: rulebook.md:4619-4621 — "the result sets the Difficulty. The defender must either accept the Maneuver's effect, or suffer Health or Morale damage equal to the Difficulty -2" -->
3. The target must **accept your effect** (disarm, trip, persuade, flee, etc.) OR **take damage = Difficulty - 2**

> *You spend 3 Sway and roll a 5, total 8. You tell the guard: "Leave this room." He either leaves, or takes 6 Morale damage.*

### Armor and Grit

- **Armor** reduces Health damage (from weapons, sorcery). Typical: 0-2.
- **Grit** reduces Morale damage (from fear, persuasion). You have Grit 1 if you filled out your gear.
- These subtract from incoming damage every time you're hit.

### Teamwork Attacks

On your turn, instead of attacking yourself, you can give your damage to an ally's attack. Their successful hit uses YOUR damage type. This lets a Sway specialist contribute Health damage through a Warfare ally, or vice versa.

## Health and Morale Tracks

### Health (Physical)
<!-- source: rulebook.md:4934-4937 (Health table), 5149-5152 (Morale table) -->

| Pool | State | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|
| 0+ | Healthy | You're fine. Maybe bruised. |
| -1 to -5 | Hurt | Test Health (Diff 4) to stay conscious. All General tests +1 Difficulty. |
| -6 to -10 | Seriously Wounded | Another Health test. All General tests +2 Difficulty. Healing half effective. Lose 1 Health per half hour. |
| Below -10 | Defeated | Out of the fight. Attacker decides your fate. |

### Morale (Willpower)

| Pool | State | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|
| 0+ | Resilient | You're fine. |
| -1 to -5 | Unstable | Test Morale (Diff 4) to keep acting. All General tests +1 Difficulty. |
| -6 to -10 | Panicked | Another Morale test. If you fail, you choose why you can't fight (fear, shame, despair). If you pass, you keep acting at +2 Difficulty. |
| Below -10 | Broken | Out of the fight. You surrender, flee, or collapse. |

<!-- source: rulebook.md:9066-9067 — "all foes are defeated when their Health or Morale reaches 0" -->
**Enemies drop at 0** (not negative). Only heroes go negative.

### Bolstering Morale

<!-- source: rulebook.md:5260-5265 — "Every point you spend from your Sway pool restores 2 Morale points to someone else or 1 Morale point to you, split up however you want among the people listening" -->
Spend Sway points to restore Morale (as an action, in or out of combat): **1 Sway spent = 2 Morale restored** to an ally, or 1 Morale to yourself. You can split among multiple people.
<!-- source: rulebook.md:1856-1858 — "A 1-point spend of Trustworthy can restore 6 points of Morale to an ally, as long as you have at least 1 rank of the General ability Sway. If you don't, or if you're trying to restore your own low Morale, a 1-point spend restores 3 points of Morale." -->
Trustworthy is even better: 1 point restores 6 Morale to an ally (requires Sway 1+), or 3 to yourself.

## Refresh Tokens

Tokens go into a shared bowl. **Any player** can grab one at any time.

Each token **refreshes 1 General ability pool point** (not Health or Morale).

<!-- source: rulebook.md:4086-4092 — "Dropping a Mook immediately creates a 1-point Refresh token... named foe or a non-Mook enemy adds 3... a big bad, 5 tokens; and a massive and truly memorable foe, 7 tokens" -->
**How tokens appear:**
- Drop a mook → **1 token**
- Drop a named foe → **3 tokens**
- Drop a big bad → **5 tokens**
- GM discretion after non-combat challenges → **2-3 tokens per active player**

**Unused tokens vanish at end of scene.** Use them!

## Drives — Your Bonus
<!-- source: rulebook.md:1574 — "Invoke your Drives to emphasize your personality, and to gain a small bonus when it really matters." The +1 value is shown in examples at rulebook.md:4424-4427. The high fantasy variant (NOT used here) changes this to a reroll per rules-high-fantasy-guide.md:74. -->

When one of your three Drives is relevant, you can **add +1** to any die result (attack, damage, ability test). Describe how your Drive motivates you. Use this when it really matters.

> *Your Drive is "Protecting the helpless." A child is in danger. You add +1 to your Warfare attack.*

## Quick Reference

| I want to... | I use... | I roll... |
|--------------|----------|-----------|
| Find information or clues | Investigative ability (no roll!) | Nothing — auto-succeed |
| Get a bonus in combat or extra cool effect | Investigative spend (1 point) | Nothing — just spend |
| Attack physically | Warfare (General) | d6 + spend vs Health Threshold |
| Attack socially | Sway (General) | d6 + spend vs Morale Threshold |
| Attack with magic | Sorcery (General) | d6 + spend vs Health or Morale Threshold |
| Dodge an attack | Athletics (General, need rank 8+) | d6 + spend, must exceed attacker's result |
| Sneak | Stealth (General) | d6 + spend vs Difficulty |
| Have the right gear | Preparedness (General) | d6 + spend vs Difficulty |
| Heal someone | Bind Wounds (General) | Spend 1 point → heal 2 Health |
| Pick a lock / steal | Burglary (General) | d6 + spend vs Difficulty |
| Force a choice on someone | Maneuver (any combat ability) | d6 + spend → sets Difficulty |

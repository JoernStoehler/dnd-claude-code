# Sorcery Cheat Sheet

*For the Sorcerer player. Print alongside the Sorcerer character template.*

---

## Two Abilities, Two Jobs

<!-- source: rulebook.md:6173 — "Sorcery is a combat General ability that targets either Health or Morale" -->
<!-- source: rulebook.md:6181-6182 — "Corruption is the Investigative ability that gives your magic its true reality-bending power" -->
**Sorcery** (General ability) = your attack power. Spend points, roll d6, deal damage. Works just like Warfare or Sway but targets either Health or Morale (you chose which when creating your character).

**Corruption** (Investigative ability) = your true reality-bending power. Spend pool points for spectacular, unique effects beyond normal attacks. These points are rare — they only refresh at the end of the adventure.

---

## What Sorcery Can Do (Without Spending Corruption)
<!-- source: rulebook.md:6164-6199 — Sorcery Quick Reference sidebar -->

Everything below is "free" — you just spend Sorcery points like any General ability:

<!-- source: rulebook.md:4319-4320 — "Select a target within Medium range, or Long range with a Difficulty of +2" -->
- **Attack** a foe at up to Medium range. Roll d6 + Sorcery spent vs their Threshold. Damage = 1d6+1. Minimum damage die = points spent (cap 6).
<!-- source: rulebook.md:6176 — "describe any unopposed, regular activity that most people could do as coming from Sorcery" -->
- **Describe any normal activity as coming from magic.** Lighting a fire, lifting something, creating a breeze — if a normal person could do it, you can describe it as sorcery.
<!-- source: rulebook.md:6179 — "create standard Maneuvers" -->
- **Maneuvers** — force a target to accept an effect or take damage. "I create an illusion of snakes around his feet — he either freezes in place or takes Morale damage."
- **At Sorcery 8+:** If you exceed the target's Threshold by 3+, your **Blast** Talent lets you hit additional nearby foes.

## What Corruption Can Do (Costs Corruption Points)
<!-- source: rulebook.md:6184-6191 — list of Corruption spend effects -->

When you need to do something magic CAN'T normally do, spend Corruption pool points:

- **Affect multiple targets at once** (beyond Blast)
- **Do exceptional, rule-breaking damage**
- **Create an area effect** (fill a room with shadow, set a building on fire)
- **Unique spells** that defy normal rules (fly, turn invisible, melt a wall, stop someone's heart)
- **Powerful Maneuvers** that you couldn't otherwise justify
- **Level a Curse** on a foe (persistent, nasty, hard to remove)

### The Rule: Your spells must fit your Spheres.
<!-- source: rulebook.md:6261 — "any spells or effects you can't rationalize within one of your Spheres are barred to you" -->

If your Spheres are Fire, Shadow, and Decay, you can't create an ice storm. You CAN describe a shadow that burns, or a fire that rots what it touches. Be creative within your theme.

### How many Corruption points to spend?

- 1 point: A significant magical effect (fly for a scene, sense all blood within a building)
- 2 points: A powerful magical effect (animate a dead body, create a wall of fire)
- 3+ points: A spectacular magical effect (raise a storm, collapse a building, curse someone for life)

The GM will tell you the cost, or you can suggest and negotiate. **You have 3 Corruption points for the entire adventure. They do not refresh. Spend them on moments that matter.**

## The Cost of Corruption — EVERY TIME You Spend
<!-- source: rulebook.md:7085-7086 — "every time you spend points of Corruption, you must choose one of these two choices" -->

Every time you spend 1 or more Corruption points in a single action, you MUST choose one:

### Internalize It (Changes Your Body)
<!-- source: rulebook.md:7088-7107 — full internalize rules -->
Make a **Health test**:
- **Difficulty = 3 + number of Corruption points you just spent**
- Spend Health points to boost your roll if needed
<!-- source: rulebook.md:7093-7097 — succeed: "choose and write down one small thing that has changed on your body. These changes are not easily noticeable by others and have no mechanical effect" -->
- If you **succeed**: a subtle change — a toenail falls off, a wart appears. Not easily noticeable. No mechanical effect.
<!-- source: rulebook.md:7105-7107 — fail: "this change is noticeable to an observer. Perhaps your pupil narrows to a slit, your tongue becomes forked... While this has no mechanical game effect" -->
- If you **fail**: a visible change — slit pupils, forked tongue, changed skin. Still no mechanical damage, but others will notice and react.

### Externalize It (Hurts Your Friends)
<!-- source: rulebook.md:7134-7136 — "allies (and only allies) within Medium range or closer must make a Morale test with a Difficulty of 3 plus 1 per Corruption point you spent. If they fail the Morale test, they lose 4 Morale." -->
The world around you warps. All allies within **Medium range** (~30m) must make a **Morale test**:
- **Difficulty = 3 + number of Corruption points you just spent**
- If they **fail**: they each lose **4 Morale**
- If they **succeed**: they feel uneasy but take no damage

This represents the horror of watching reality twist. Talk to your party before externalizing — some characters have high Morale and can absorb it.

## Your Spheres
<!-- source: rulebook.md:6258-6265 — "Create your own Sorcerous Spheres or borrow and customize one of these. You gain one Sphere for every rank of Corruption you possess." -->
<!-- source: rulebook.md:6281-6283 — "Spheres don't set your Sorcery's raw power; they're basically just your magic's special effects" -->

Spheres are your magic's **special effects** — what your sorcery looks and feels like. They don't change your damage numbers; they change your descriptions and what creative effects you can justify.

You chose 3 Spheres when creating your character. Here's how to use them:

- When you attack, describe how the attack fits your Sphere. *"Thorns erupt from the ground and pierce his legs"* (Plants, Health). *"His dead mother's voice whispers from the shadows behind him"* (Ghosts, Morale).
- When you spend Corruption for a unique effect, the effect must fit at least one of your Spheres.
- Be creative! Spheres overlap. Fire + Decay = corrosive flames. Shadow + Fear = living nightmares.
- There is no spell list. You describe what your magic does within your Spheres, and the GM tells you the cost.

## Example Round

> You have Sorcery 8 and Corruption 3 (Spheres: Fire, Shadow, Decay). Your sorcery targets Health.
>
> **Normal attack:** "I hurl a bolt of shadow-fire at the thug." Spend 3 Sorcery, roll d6. Get a 4. Total: 7. Threshold was 3, so you hit. You also exceeded by 4, which is more than 3, so your Blast Talent activates — you can hit a second thug nearby for base damage. Damage against primary target: d6+1, minimum 3 on the die. You roll a 5. 5+1 = 6 damage, minus their Armor. Second thug takes 4 damage (base).
>
> **Corruption spend:** "I want to collapse the warehouse wall onto the guards." GM says that's 2 Corruption points. You describe how decay spreads through the supports as shadow-fire eats the mortar. The wall collapses. You choose to **internalize**: Health test at Difficulty 5 (3+2). You spend 2 Health and roll a 4. Total 6 — you succeed. You write down "fingernails turned black" on your character sheet.

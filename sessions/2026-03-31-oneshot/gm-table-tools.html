Balance Levers & Challenge Generator

Combat Balance — Adjust On The Fly

Too easy

  • Spend more Malus on attacks (+1 to hit per point, raises min damage)
  • A wave of 2-3 mooks arrives (reinforcements, attracted by noise)
  • Named foe uses a Special Ability they haven't triggered yet
  • Environment changes: fire, collapsing floor, rising water, darkness
  • The enemy was holding back — reveal a hidden weapon or second form

Too hard

  • Stop spending Malus — enemies just use static attack bonus
  • Mooks flee or surrender once half are down
  • Named foe tries to escape instead of fighting to death
  • Drop extra Refresh tokens: "a moment to catch your breath"
  • Quietly reduce enemy Health/Morale — nobody knows but you
  • An ally shows up (someone the heroes helped earlier, a Watch patrol)

Investigation Balance

Stalled — players don't know where to go

  • Make the next clue passive — just narrate it when they enter a scene
  • An NPC volunteers info ("You look like you're investigating those murders...")
  • The timeline advances — Sialia kills again, creating a fresh crime scene
  • A player's Allegiance pays off — an ally sends a message with a lead
  • Cut to a montage: "You spend an hour asking around. Here's what you find."

Too easy — players solving everything too fast

  • A complication arrives: the Cleaners, a Watch patrol, an enemy from a player's sheet
  • The clue raises a new question instead of just answering one
  • An NPC lies (and players with Liar's Tell notice — but what's the truth?)
  • The next scene has a social obstacle before the clue is accessible

Pacing — Scene Dragging?

  • Something interrupts: a noise, a newcomer, a fight breaking out nearby
  • "You've learned what you can here. Where next?"
  • Jump to the consequence: "Ten minutes later, you're at the warehouse. What do you do?"
  • Use a player's Enemy — they show up at the worst moment

Ad-Hoc Challenges — When Players Go Off-Script

Break into somewhereBurglary test (target 4). Maybe Stealth too. What's inside? (Decide when they enter.)What's behind the door: empty room, guards, a clue, a trap

Convince a crowdSway test, maybe multi-target with Play to the CrowdWhat the crowd does if convinced: riot, scatter, help, become witnesses

Do something not covered abovePick the closest General ability. Target 4 (easy) to 8 (very hard). Describe consequences of failure.Failure doesn't have to mean "nothing happens" — it can mean "you succeed but..."

The trick: you don't decide what's behind the door / on the belt / in the letter until the players look. Keep options open. Say "yes" to the action, then decide the consequences.

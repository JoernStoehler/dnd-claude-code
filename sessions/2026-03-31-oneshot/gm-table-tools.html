Balance Levers — Adjust Mid-Scene

Combat too easy?

  • Spend more Malus on attacks (each point of Malus = +1 to hit, min damage floor rises)
  • Add a wave of mooks (2-3 more arrive)
  • Named foe uses a Special Ability you haven't triggered yet
  • Environment changes: fire, collapsing floor, rising water

Combat too hard?

  • Stop spending Malus — enemies just use their static attack bonus
  • Mooks flee or surrender when half are dropped
  • Named foe tries to escape instead of fighting to the death
  • Drop extra Refresh tokens: "you find a moment to catch your breath"
  • Reduce enemy Health/Morale on the fly — nobody knows the numbers but you

Investigation stalled?

  • Make the next clue passive — just tell them when they enter the scene
  • An NPC volunteers information unprompted ("You look like you're investigating those murders...")
  • The timeline advances — Sialia kills someone new, creating a fresh crime scene with new clues
  • A player's Allegiance pays off — an ally sends a message with a lead

Scene dragging?

  • Something interrupts: a noise, a newcomer, a complication
  • "You've learned what you can here. Where to next?"
  • Cut to a montage: "You spend an hour asking around. Here's what you find..."

What's On That Belt? — Ad-Hoc Items

When an NPC has "something interesting" and you haven't decided what:

#ItemWhy it matters (or doesn't)
1A key to somewhereYou decide where later
2A sealed letterFrom someone relevant, or not
3A vial of somethingPoison? Medicine? Perfume?
4A religious tokenDenari, or a small god?
5A coin purse (Wealth 1)Someone paid them recently
6A crude weaponThey expected trouble
7A scrap of paper with an addressPoints to a node the players haven't visited
8Nothing — false alarmJust a weird buckle

You don't have to decide what it is until the players examine it. Even then, you can stall: "It's sealed. You'd need Skulduggery or Burglary to open it without damaging the contents."

Sorcerer Quick Reference

1. Your magic looks like your Spheres (check your sheet — e.g. Fire, Shadow, Decay). Describe effects using them.
2. Small magic: spend Sorcery points, roll d6 + spent vs target's Threshold.
3. Big magic: spend Corruption points (you have as many as your Corruption rank — they don't refresh), then choose a side effect — your body changes, or allies take Morale damage.

Your Spheres

You have 1 Sphere per Corruption rank (check your sheet). They define what your magic looks like — not how powerful it is. All effects must fit at least one Sphere.

  • Fire + Decay = corrosive flames. Shadow + Fear = living nightmares. Combine creatively.
  • If you can't justify it through a Sphere, you can't do it.

Check your sheet: your Sorcery damages either Health (body) or Morale (mind) — you chose at creation.

Attacking with Sorcery

Normal attack
Up to Medium range (~30m). Spend Sorcery points → roll d6 + spent vs target's Threshold (the number to hit, usually 3–4). Damage: 1d6 + 1.
Minimum damage
Your d6 damage roll can't be lower than the points you spent (capped at 6). Spend 3 → d6 is at least 3.
Blast (Sorcery 8+)
If your roll exceeds the Threshold by 3+ → hit one additional nearby foe. Extra foe takes base damage (1d6+1).
Out of Sorcery points
You still attack — roll bare d6 vs Threshold, no minimum damage floor.
Maneuvers
Instead of damage: force a choice. Your roll total becomes a Difficulty — target accepts your effect (blind, restrain, terrify) or takes total − 2 as damage.
Attack example: "Shadow-fire bolt." Spend 3 Sorcery, roll 4 = total 7 vs Threshold 3. Hit, exceeded by 4 → Blast hits a second thug. Primary target: d6+1, min die is 3 (you spent 3). Roll 5 → 5+1 = 6 damage minus their Armor. Second thug: base damage = 1d6+1.

Big Magic (Corruption Spends)

Your Corruption pool = your Corruption rank. These don't refresh until adventure's end.

What you can do
  • Affect multiple targets beyond Blast
  • Area effects — fill a room with shadow, ignite a building
  • Unique effects — fly, invisibility, melt a wall, stop a heart
  • Powerful Maneuvers you couldn't otherwise justify
  • Curses — persistent, hard to remove
Cost
1 pointSignificant — fly for a scene, sense blood in a building
2 pointsPowerful — animate a corpse, wall of fire
3 pointsSpectacular — collapse a building, curse for life
Describe the effect; GM sets the cost. Big magic doesn't need a Sorcery roll — just the Corruption spend and a side effect.
Big magic example: "I collapse the warehouse wall onto the guards." GM says 2 Corruption. Describe: decay spreads through the supports as shadow-fire eats the mortar. Wall falls. Then choose a side effect (see below).

Side Effects — Every Corruption Spend

Every time you spend Corruption → choose one. Internalize always changes your body (the roll determines if others notice). Externalize hurts allies' Morale instead.

Internalize (your body changes)

Roll d6 vs Difficulty 3 + points spent. You can spend Health to boost the roll.

PassSubtle change — wart, toenail falls off. Others won't notice. No game effect.
FailVisible change — slit pupils, forked tongue, cracked skin. No damage, but others see it and react.

Externalize (allies take Morale damage)

All allies within ~30m roll d6 vs Difficulty 3 + points spent.

PassUneasy. No damage.
FailLose 4 Morale each.

Warn your allies before choosing this.

Side effect example (from above): You spent 2 Corruption. Internalize: Health test Diff 5 (3+2). Spend 2 Health, roll 4 = total 6. Pass. Write "fingernails turned black" on your sheet.

GM Reference — One-Shot

Opening the Session (19:00)

Pitch (30 seconds)
"This is a sword & sorcery game. Corrupt city, dangerous magic, morally gray. You're heroes-for-hire. You need one d6. Target number is 4. If you have the right ability, you get the clue — no roll."
Hand out
Blank character sheet + character creation guide + ability lookup + player rules reference. Sorcery cheat sheet if someone picks sorcerer. Pregen examples for inspiration.
Character creation (10 min)
Walk through creation guide steps 1–10 with the group. Answer questions from your GM reference (this sheet). Tell them investigative points by player count. Remind them: leave points unspent if unsure.
Scene 0 (19:15)
Start with the ambush. Tell players: "Any points you spend in this fight, you get back after." This kills spend-fear and teaches combat.

Character Creation — Answering Questions

How many Investigative Build Points?
3 players: 12 · 4 players: 11 · 5+ players: 10. Plus 3 additional for Allegiances (2 Allies, 1 Enemy) — these are extra, not from the ability pool.
Single profession bonus?
If ALL profession picks (not Social, not Allegiances) come from one profession → +1 free point. Spend on any Social ability, Allegiance, or same-profession ability.
How many General Build Points?
30 for abilities. 18 split between Health and Morale (min 3 each). Max ability = 2× next-highest.
What does rank 8+ do?
Unlocks a Talent — a permanent bonus ability. See ability lookup sheet. Key talents: Dodge (Athletics), Flashback (Preparedness), Cleave/Blast/Play to the Crowd (multi-target attacks).
What's a Threshold?
The number enemies must roll to hit you (Health Threshold) or demoralize you (Morale Threshold). Default 3, becomes 4 if that stat is 10+.
What's Armor vs Grit?
Armor subtracts from Health damage every hit. Grit subtracts from Morale damage every hit. Grit 1 from having 5+ gear items. Armor from wearing actual armor (leather=1, chain=2).
Do I need Sorcery?
Only if you have Corruption 1+. Sorcery is the General ability for magic attacks. Corruption is the Investigative ability that fuels big magic. No Corruption = no magic.
What should I pick?
At least one combat ability (Warfare, Sway, or Sorcery) at 5+. At 8+ you get a great Talent. Beyond that, whatever sounds fun.
Can I change my mind?
Yes. You can freely redistribute all points after your first session. During this session, unspent points can be assigned any time.

Running the Game — Rules Lookup

Clue delivery

Player has the right ability + right situation
They get the clue. No roll. Tell them. Adjust delivery based on pacing — if they're stuck, volunteer clues. If they're sharp, make them ask.
Player doesn't have the ability but tries something creative
Reward it. "Permissive clue-finding" — if it's plausible, give the information. Prepared clues are a safety net, not a straitjacket.
Investigative spend — judging the effect
1 point = a meaningful spotlight moment. 2+ points = something dramatic. If no interesting effect exists, tell the player and they keep their points. Never penalize a failed spend attempt.

Tests

Difficulty
Default 4. Easy: 3. Hard: 5-6. Very hard: 7-8. Near impossible: 9+. Don't go above 8 unless you want it to feel desperate.
Retries
Can't retry unless circumstances change. New approach, new information, or someone helps.
Cooperation
One leader rolls. Each helper spends 1 point from the same ability (or a relevant different one). Leader gets +1 per helper, minus 1.
Piggybacking
Group test — leader rolls, everyone else pays 1 point. If someone's pool is 0, Difficulty rises by 2 (or leave them behind).

Combat

Initiative
You pick who goes first. After each turn, that person picks the next (friend or foe). Last person picks who opens the next round.
Attack
d6 + spend vs Threshold (usually 3-4). Hit → 1d6 + Damage Modifier. Min damage die = points spent (capped at 6).
Critical hit
Exceed Threshold by 5+ → extra 1d6 damage.
Multi-target (Cleave/Blast/Play to the Crowd)
Need rank 8+. Exceed Threshold by 3+ → hit one extra foe at base damage (1d6+1). Every further +3 = another. Can't crit AND multi-target same attack.
Investigative spend in combat
Offensive: +3 to attack roll OR +1d6 damage. Defensive: Armor 4 or Grit 4 for 1 round, or +3 Threshold vs one attack. Player must describe how the ability applies.
Maneuver
Roll attack. Total becomes Difficulty. Target: accept effect (disarm, trip, persuade, flee) or take total−2 as damage. Armor/Grit may reduce. Mental effects last the scene; physical effects last until end of next round.
Teamwork attack
Skip your attack. Ally's hit uses your damage type (1d6, no modifier, but your Investigative/Critical dice carry over). Lets Sway contribute to a Warfare hit.
Dodge (Athletics 8+)
After being hit, before damage: spend 1+ Athletics, roll. Must exceed attacker's roll. With shield: only match. If successful: attack is cancelled entirely.
Bolster Morale
As an action: 1 Sway spent = 2 Morale to ally (1 to self). Can split. Trustworthy: 1 pt = 6 Morale to ally (needs Sway 1+), 3 to self.
Bind Wounds
1 point spent = 2 Health healed to someone else. To self: only 1 Health (unless rank 8+, then 2).

Damage & defeat

Enemies at 0 Health or Morale
Defeated. You narrate what happens. Mooks just drop. Named foes may try to flee.
Heroes at 0
Fine — still Healthy/Resilient.
Heroes at −1 to −5
Hurt/Unstable. Test d6 vs Diff 4 to stay conscious/active. +1 to all General test Difficulties.
Heroes at −6 to −10
Seriously Wounded/Panicked. Test again. +2 to Difficulties. Healing halved (Health). Choose why you can't fight (Morale).
Heroes below −10
Defeated/Broken. Out of the fight. You decide consequences — captivity is more fun than death for a one-shot.

Refresh tokens

When to award
Mook dropped: 1. Named foe: 3. Big bad: 5. After non-combat challenge: 2-3 per active player. Put in shared bowl.
What they do
Any player, any time (except mid-spend): take 1 token = refresh 1 General pool point (not Health/Morale). Unused tokens vanish at end of scene.
Scene 0 special
All pools fully refresh after Scene 0. Tell players this upfront.

Drives

When a player invokes a relevant Drive: +1 to any die result. The rulebook says "when it really matters" — no hard frequency limit. For a one-shot, letting each player use it a few times is fine.

Point Economy — How Spending Should Feel

The system is designed around aggressive spending. Hoarding is a trap — unspent points at adventure's end are wasted.

General ability points (Warfare, Sway, Sorcery, Athletics, etc.)
Spend freely. They come back via Refresh tokens (1 per mook dropped, 3 per named foe, 5 per big bad). The game's intended loop: spend big → drop enemies → collect tokens → spend again. Spending 3-5 points on one attack is normal, not reckless. Mooks refund themselves.
Investigative points (Charm, Know Monstrosities, Skulduggery, etc.)
Do not refresh during the adventure. But they're meant to be spent — every unspent point is a missed +1d6 damage or +3 on a roll. Encourage players to spend 1-2 per fight scene and 1-2 during investigation. In the climax, spend everything that's left.
Corruption points (sorcerer only)
Also don't refresh. These are the sorcerer's "big moments" — 1-2 spectacular effects per adventure is the intended pace.
Health and Morale
These are NOT spendable resources in normal play — they're damage tracks. Players spend from them only on Health/Morale tests (staying conscious, resisting fear). Spending Health to boost a Health test is risky — it's paying HP to not fall unconscious, which can spiral. Morale works the same way.
Scene 0 rule: Tell players at the start: "Any points you spend in this fight, you get ALL of them back after." This breaks the hoarding instinct immediately. They learn what spending feels like when it's risk-free, and they carry that behavior into the rest of the game.

As GM — when to nudge players:

  • "You still have 6 Warfare points — this is a good time to spend big" (mid-combat, against a tough foe)
  • "Remember you can spend an Investigative point for +1d6 damage" (after a hit, when they forget)
  • "There are Refresh tokens in the bowl" (players forget to take them)
  • "This is the last fight — spend everything you've got" (climax scene)

As GM — Malus (your spending resource):

  • Malus is the enemy equivalent of General ability pools. Spend it to boost attacks, trigger Special Abilities, score crits, or improvise.
  • Start low — use 0-2 Malus per attack in early fights. Save big Malus spends for dramatic moments (a villain's signature move).
  • Sialia (night form) has Malus 18 — that's a LOT. Don't spend it all at once. 2-3 per round on attacks, save 6 for Fluid if she needs to escape, save 3 for Grab on a dramatic moment.

Sorcery & Corruption

Normal sorcery attack
Same as Warfare/Sway — spend Sorcery, roll d6, beat Threshold. No Corruption cost. Range: Medium.
Big magic (Corruption spend)
Player describes effect, you set cost (1 = significant, 2 = powerful, 3 = spectacular). No roll needed — just the spend + a side effect.
Side effects — every Corruption spend
Internalize: Health test, Diff 3 + points spent. Pass = subtle body change. Fail = visible change. No extra damage either way.
Externalize: All allies within Medium range make Morale test, Diff 3 + points. Fail = lose 4 Morale.
Corruption points left?
They have 3 for the whole adventure. Don't refresh. Precious resource — they should spend 1-2 during the session.

World & Cast — Names You'll Need

The City

Eversink — the Sinking City. Canal city on islands in a river delta. ~100,000 people. Slowly sinks into the swamp (~8cm/year). Old buildings half-submerged. Bells everywhere. Brightly painted, beautiful, corrupt.
Denari — the goddess. Patron of commerce and civilization. Every exchange of money is a prayer to her. Her church runs moneylending, inquisitors, and government. Sorcery weakens her power — so sorcery is nearly illegal.
The Triskadane — 13 anonymous rulers of Eversink + their secret police. The real government behind the public committees.

The Setting: Harbor Approach

Working-class dockside district. Warehouses, chandlers, rope-makers, cheap taverns, fish markets. Smells of salt, tar, low tide. A protection racket has been bleeding the shopkeepers for years — everyone knows, nobody talks.
Cutter's Lane — dead-end street backing onto a canal. Murano's Pickles is the last shop. Canal-side door for deliveries by barge.
The Rusty Anchor — dockside tavern where workers drink. Good for gossip.

Flavor You Can Drop In

  • Bells ring constantly — from temples, boats, bridges
  • Statues everywhere, on every building — they represent souls
  • Canals reek in summer; gondolas are common transport
  • The Watch patrols at night, half-asleep
  • Beggars sell "blessing stones" (pebbles) — Denari forbids charity, so exchanging a pebble for a coin counts as trade
  • Buildings visibly sinking — doors below street level, ground floors flooded
  • Wild swans paddle under bridges
  • Pennants: deep blue with a white swan over a gold coin

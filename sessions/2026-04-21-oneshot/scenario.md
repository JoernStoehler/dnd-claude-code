# The Sin-Drinker: One-Shot for Monday 2026-04-21

Adapted from Kevin Kulp's adventure seed. Designed for 3-5 players new to SotS, 19:00-22:00.

**System:** Swords of the Serpentine (vanilla, no high fantasy variant)
**Setting:** Eversink (the default city)
**Tier:** Standard heroes (18 Health/Morale, 30 General, 10-12 Investigative)

---

## The Situation

**Watch Commander Antonio Teldi** runs protection rackets in Harbor Approach. He's corrupt, cowardly, and greedy. His criminal associates have been dying one by one — murdered by something that drains blood and leaves a vinegar smell. Teldi can't go through official channels because doing so would expose his crimes. So he hires outsiders: the Heroes.

**Sialia Murano** is a penanggalan — a cursed woman whose head and viscera detach at night to hunt. She feeds on the wicked. By day she runs Murano's Pickles with her wife Lara, soaking her organs in vinegar to fit back into her body. She is a vigilante, not a mindless monster. She is systematically killing Teldi's criminal network because they've been preying on Harbor Approach's people.

**The heroes don't know any of this.** They're told: "a monster is killing people in Harbor Approach. Find it, kill it." As they investigate, they discover the victims were criminals, the employer is corrupt, and the "monster" has a moral code. What they do with this information is entirely up to them.

### What happens if the heroes do nothing (timeline)

- **Night 1 (tonight):** Sialia kills Belto, the poison dealer, at his warehouse. (This has just happened as the adventure starts.)
- **Night 2 (tomorrow):** Sialia targets Grazza, Teldi's bookkeeper, at her home.
- **Night 3:** Sialia goes after Teldi himself.

The heroes have roughly 2 in-game days before the situation resolves itself violently. Teldi gets increasingly desperate. If heroes are slow, they arrive at locations to find fresh corpses instead of living witnesses.

## Scene 0: Ambush in the Alley (INTRO — Action Opener)

**Purpose:** Teach combat rules. Get players rolling dice immediately. Late arrivals can join mid-fight.

**Situation:** The heroes are walking through Harbor Approach at dusk, heading to meet a potential employer. Three hooded thugs step out of an alley — they work for someone who doesn't want the heroes poking around in Harbor Approach tonight. (Who sent them? Teldi, testing the heroes' mettle before hiring them. Or one of Teldi's rivals. It doesn't matter — these thugs don't know who hired them.)

**Toolkit:**
- 3 Dockside Thugs (scale: +1 thug per hero above 3)
- Narrow alley, crates and barrels for cover, a canal on one side
- One thug has a crossbow on a rooftop (Close range), two on the ground with clubs (Point-Blank)
- A beggar watches from across the canal

**Stat Block — Dockside Thug (Mook)**

Surly, desperate, expendable

- Defense — Health: Health Threshold 3, Armor 0, Health 1
- Defense — Morale: Morale Threshold 3, Grit 0, Morale 1
- Offense — Warfare: +0; Fixed Damage 3 (club) or Fixed Damage 2 (crossbow)
- Offense — Sway: +0; Fixed Damage 2 (threats)
- Abilities: Malus 3
- Refresh Tokens: 1

**GM Notes:**
- Tell players upfront: "Any points you spend in this fight, you get back after." This removes spend-fear and teaches the system.
- Use this fight to demonstrate: attack rolls, damage, Health/Morale tracks, Sway attacks (a thug might yell "Surrender or die!" as a Maneuver), and Investigative spends for bonus damage.
- The fight should last 2-3 rounds max. Mooks drop fast.
- If a player has Sway 8+, let them try talking a thug into fleeing — demonstrate social combat works.
- The beggar across the canal can provide a lead to Teldi if the heroes don't already have one.

**After the fight:** All General ability pools refresh. Transition to the START node.

## START: Teldi's Office (CORE)

**Situation:** Watch Commander Antonio Teldi meets the heroes at a small Watch station in Harbor Approach. It's after hours, quiet. He's alone — he specifically didn't bring other Watch officers. He's sweating. He offers a Badge of Office (lets heroes act with Watch authority for 48 hours) and payment of Wealth 3 on delivery of the monster's head.

**Teldi's story (what he tells the heroes):** "Something is killing people in my district. Two dead in the last week. Not normal murders — blood drained, bodies mutilated. I need this thing found and killed before it strikes again. The Watch is stretched thin. I can't spare officers. That's why I'm hiring you."

**What Teldi doesn't say:** The victims are his criminal associates. He knows exactly who they are and why they might be targeted. He's terrified he's next.

**Toolkit — Antonio Teldi:**
- Appears: tired, stressed, authoritative but rattled
- Wants: the killer dead, his crimes unexposed
- Has: Watch authority, Badge of Office, knowledge he won't share willingly
- Will lie about: his relationship to the victims ("just citizens in my district"), why he's not using his own officers ("stretched thin")
- Breaks under: evidence of his corruption, Intimidation, Sway Maneuver where the damage of refusing exceeds his Morale

**Stat Block — Watch Commander Antonio Teldi** (only relevant if heroes fight him later)

Corrupt, cowardly, shrewd, self-preserving

- Defense — Health: Health Threshold 3, Armor 1 (leather under shirt), Health 8
- Defense — Morale: Morale Threshold 3, Grit 1 (bluster), Morale 6
- Offense — Warfare: +1; Damage Modifier +1 (truncheon)
- Offense — Sway: +1; Damage Modifier +1 (false authority)
- Abilities: Malus 12
- Special Abilities: Allies (cost 3 — City Watch, but he won't use this unless desperate as it draws attention), Summoning (cost 3 — 2 corrupt Watch Officers he trusts)
- Misc: Alertness Modifier +1
- Refresh Tokens: 3

**Note:** Teldi is NOT a combat encounter. He's a social puzzle. He's the employer. Combat with Teldi probably happens in the resolution, if ever.

### Clues at this node

**Passive (anyone gets these):**
- Teldi gives the heroes the names and locations of the two victims: Zarra (a "trader" found dead in her room) and Belto (a "merchant" found dead in his warehouse tonight). → Leads to **Node A** and **Node C**.
- Teldi mentions Harbor Approach is the area to investigate — the killings are local. → Leads to **Node B**.

**Active (require ability + asking):**

| Ability | Clue | Points to |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Laws & Traditions, Vigilance | Teldi's Badge of Office is real, but this meeting is irregular — no paperwork, no witnesses, no official request. This is off the books. | Teldi is hiding something (flags his corruption) |
| Liar's Tell, Charm | Teldi flinches when naming the victims. He knows them personally, not just as "citizens." | Teldi's connection to victims |
| Skulduggery, Scurrilous Rumors | You've heard of Zarra — she's a mercenary enforcer, not a "trader." And Belto deals in illegal poisons. These aren't innocent citizens. | Victims are criminals → Node A, C with context |
| City's Secrets | Harbor Approach has been rumored to have a protection racket for years. The Watch turns a blind eye. | Teldi's racket |
| Corruption, Spirit Sight | There's a faint residue of something old and hungry in this district — not sorcery exactly, but a curse. Something supernatural is at work. | Supernatural element (atmosphere) |

## Node A: Zarra's Murder Scene (CORE)

**Situation:** A rented room above a chandler's shop in Harbor Approach. Zarra was a mercenary enforcer who collected protection money for Teldi. She was killed three nights ago. The room is sealed by the Watch (Teldi's order — he doesn't want anyone finding what's here). The chandler downstairs, **Old Piet**, saw nothing but heard the struggle.

**Toolkit:**
- Locked door (Burglary Difficulty 3, or the Badge of Office opens it, or just kick it in)
- One-room apartment: bed, table, weapons rack, chest of personal effects
- Piet the chandler: elderly, nervous, wants to be left alone, heard "thrashing and a wet tearing sound" three nights ago around midnight
- Zarra's body has been removed by the Watch, but blood, evidence, and smell remain

### Clues at this node

**Passive:**
- The room reeks faintly of vinegar. Blood is everywhere — walls, ceiling. Something tore this woman apart.
- Long red hairs on the floor near the window. Not Zarra's (she had black hair).

**Active:**

| Ability | Clue | Points to |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Leechcraft | Zarra was drained of blood through small puncture wounds in her neck and torso. The mutilation happened after the blood was drained. Cause of death: exsanguination. | Bloodsucking creature |
| Spot Frailty, Tactics of Death | Zarra fought back hard — she was a professional. Knife marks on the walls. But the attacker came through the window and struck from above. The fight was over fast. | Attacker is very strong, can fly or climb walls |
| Vigilance, Skulduggery | Zarra's chest has a false bottom. Inside: a ledger listing protection payments collected from shops in Harbor Approach, along with Teldi's initials. This is the protection racket's accounting. | **Confirms Teldi's corruption.** Also lists other names: Belto, Grazza. → Node C + Grazza as future target |
| Know Monstrosities | The combination of blood draining, puncture wounds, and vinegar scent matches legends of a penanggalan — a cursed creature whose head detaches at night to hunt. Vulnerable only when the headless body is found and destroyed. The vinegar is used to shrink the organs to fit back in the body by day. | Identifies the creature type. Vinegar → Node D |
| City's Secrets, Scurrilous Rumors | Old Piet mentions that "the pickle woman" was asking about Zarra a week ago. Wanted to know where she lived, her habits. Nice woman, red hair. Runs the pickle shop on Cutter's Lane. | **Direct lead to Node D** |
| Charm, Servility (spend, talking to Piet) | Piet opens up: "This whole street pays protection to the Watch. Everyone knows. Zarra collected. Nobody's sorry she's dead, just scared of what killed her." | Motive context |

## Node B: Harbor Approach Streets (ALTERNATE)

**Situation:** Not a single location but a montage of the neighborhood. The heroes walk the streets, talk to locals, visit taverns, observe the district. Harbor Approach is a working-class dockside neighborhood — warehouses, chandlers, rope-makers, cheap taverns, fish markets. It smells of salt, tar, and low tide.

**Purpose:** Atmosphere. Social interaction. Redundant clues. Demonstrates investigative ability use without combat. Can be done as a quick montage or played out fully, depending on time.

**Toolkit — People of Harbor Approach:**
- **Dock workers** at the Rusty Anchor tavern: scared, resentful of the Watch, have heard rumors
- **A street preacher** on a canal bridge: ranting about sin and punishment, knows more than expected
- **Shop owners** along the main street: tight-lipped about the protection racket unless they trust the heroes
- **Grazza**, Teldi's bookkeeper, if the heroes have her name from Node A: a nervous middle-aged woman who handles Teldi's legitimate paperwork. She knows everything. She's also next on Sialia's list.

### Clues at this node

**Passive:**
- Harbor Approach is tense. People don't linger after dark. Two murders in a week has everyone on edge.
- A distinct vinegar-and-brine smell comes from a shop on Cutter's Lane — Murano's Pickles. (Pointing it out as "distinctive" is enough; it may mean nothing yet or might connect to vinegar smell from murder scenes.)

**Active:**

| Ability | Clue | Points to |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Scurrilous Rumors | The dock workers know Zarra and Belto were Teldi's people. "Everyone knows about the racket. Nobody talks about it." Also: "There's a third one — Grazza, handles his money. She lives above the counting house on Salt Street." | Confirms corruption + Grazza as target |
| Charm, Trustworthy | A shopkeeper opens up: "Whoever's doing this — some folk think they're a hero, not a monster. These people being killed? They're the ones who've been bleeding us dry for years." | Moral texture — the "monster" has supporters |
| Intimidation, Command | A nervous informant admits: "I saw something three nights ago. A light in the sky, like a lantern, floating over the rooftops toward Zarra's place. But lanterns don't trail... things... behind them." | Penanggalan sighting (flying head with dangling viscera) → supernatural clue |
| City's Secrets | Cutter's Lane is a dead-end street backing onto a canal. Murano's Pickles is the last shop. The canal-side wall has a door — goods come in by barge. Only way out other than the front. | Tactical intel for Node D |
| Spirit Sight | The streets of Harbor Approach are stained with old misery — but around one particular shop on Cutter's Lane, there's a halo of something older and stranger. A curse, ancient and alive. You sense the curse is tied to a living person — it sustains itself by feeding on guilt. If the host body were destroyed while the curse was separated from it, the curse would unravel. | → Node D + kill method |
| Corruption | You can feel Corruption here — not sorcery, but something adjacent. A curse that sustains itself by feeding. It's strongest near Cutter's Lane. | → Node D |
| Scurrilous Rumors, Servility | A dock worker mentions: "You might want to check what happened at the warehouse on the harbor — Belto's place. Something bad there too, I heard." | → **Node C** (cross-link) |

Old soldiers' tales say the only way to kill one is to find and destroy its empty body while the head is out hunting. | Creature ID + kill method (redundant with Node A) |

Grazza is terrified. She knows about the murders and suspects she's next. She knows everything about Teldi's operation but is too scared to talk freely. A Sway Maneuver (Persuade) or evidence of Teldi's crimes loosens her tongue. She can confirm:
- Teldi runs the protection racket using Zarra (enforcement), Belto (poisons/threats), and herself (money)
- The victims were all Teldi's inner circle
- She's heard the killer is "something unnatural" but doesn't know what

**Grazza** — not a combatant

Terrified, meticulous, trapped

- Defense — Health: Health Threshold 3, Health 1
- Defense — Morale: Morale Threshold 3, Grit 0, Morale 4
- Offense — Sway: +0; Fixed Damage 2 (begging)
- Abilities: Malus 3

## Node C: Belto's Warehouse (CORE)

**Situation:** A warehouse on the harbor, reeking of chemicals. Belto dealt in illegal poisons — selling to thieves' guilds, noble families, and anyone who could pay. He was killed tonight. The body is fresh. The warehouse is dark, cluttered with crates and barrels.

**Complication — The Cleaners:** A thieves' guild crew (3-4 people) arrives to clean out Belto's inventory before the Watch finds it. They're not hostile unless provoked — they just want the merchandise. This is a social OR combat encounter depending on player approach.

**Toolkit:**
- Large warehouse, dark, one main door, one loading dock on the canal side
- Chemical smells mask other odors (but not completely)
- Belto's body: fat middle-aged man, slumped over a workbench. Visibly drained. Organs disturbed.
- The Cleaners arrive 5-10 minutes after the heroes (or are already here if the heroes go to Node C late)
- Crates of poisons, alchemical supplies, exotic ingredients

### Clues at this node

**Passive:**
- Belto's body: same as Zarra — puncture wounds, blood drained, faint vinegar smell. But this one is fresh. Whatever did this was here less than an hour ago.
- Prehensile tracks on the floor and ceiling — like something with tendrils crawled across surfaces.

**Active:**

| Ability | Clue | Points to |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Leechcraft | Belto was also poisoned internally — something mixed alchemical compounds inside his body. The killer didn't just drain him; she used his own poisons against him. This suggests intelligence and deliberation, not a mindless predator. | Intelligent killer with a plan |
| Know Monstrosities | The ceiling tracks confirm a penanggalan. The creature is essentially a flying head with dangling organs, and those organs leave these exact marks. They're nocturnal. By day, the penanggalan appears fully human. | → Search for someone who appears human by day and has access to vinegar (Node D) |
| Skulduggery, Felonious Intent | Belto's customer ledger is still here (the Cleaners haven't found it yet). It lists sales of poisons to Teldi — specifically, poisons used to threaten non-paying shopkeepers. More evidence of the racket. | Confirms Teldi's corruption |
| Vigilance | A distinctive vinegar-salt-herb odor under the chemical reek. Not from the poisons — it smells like pickles. Specifically, like the barrels at that shop on Cutter's Lane. | → Node D |
| Forgotten Lore | Penanggalan are not born — they're cursed. Usually the curse is punishment for a great wrong suffered. The curse feeds on the wicked. It can only be ended by destroying the cursed person's empty body while the head is separated. | How to defeat Sialia + moral context |

### The Cleaners

**Situation:** 3-4 members of a thieves' guild, here for Belto's poison inventory. They don't care about the murder — they care about the merchandise. Led by **Vess**, a pragmatic woman with no loyalty to Teldi.

**Toolkit — Vess and Cleaners:**
- Vess wants: the poisons, no trouble, no witnesses
- Vess has: guild backing (will mention "people who'll come asking" if threatened), lockpicks, a barge on the canal for loading
- Vess will cooperate if: heroes let her take the merchandise, or offer information she values, or demonstrate they're too dangerous to fight
- Vess knows: Belto worked for Teldi, the racket is common knowledge in the underworld, "someone's cleaning house and I'm not complaining"

**Social approach:** Vess is pragmatic. She'll trade information for being allowed to work. She can confirm the racket, name Grazza, and mention that "the pickle woman on Cutter's Lane asked me about Belto last week — wanted to know his schedule."

**Combat approach:** If provoked, the Cleaners fight to escape, not to kill. They'll flee toward the canal barge.

Quiet, professional, loyal to coin

Pragmatic, unflappable, sharp-eyed

## Node D: Murano's Pickles (FINALE)

**Situation:** A pickle shop on Cutter's Lane, a dead-end street backing onto a canal. By day, a functioning business run by Sialia and Lara Murano. Barrels of vinegar, brine, and pickled everything. It smells overwhelmingly of vinegar.

This is the climax — but how it plays out depends entirely on when the heroes arrive (day vs. night), what they know, and what they want to do.

**Toolkit — The Location:**
- Small shop front, counter, shelves of jars
- Back room: living quarters, a bedroom, a large soaking tub full of vinegar (for Sialia's organs)
- Canal-side door to a small dock (back exit)
- Cellar: additional barrels, root vegetables, and nothing suspicious
- The shop is on a dead-end street — front entrance or canal-side door are the only ways in/out

**Toolkit — Sialia Murano:**
- Appears (by day): a striking woman with long red hair, mid-30s, kind, tired, runs a small business
- Wants: to finish killing Teldi's people and then Teldi himself, to protect Harbor Approach, to be left alone with Lara
- Has: the penanggalan curse (she was cursed years ago after her first husband was murdered by criminals who were never punished — the curse came from her grief and rage), physical power at night, vulnerability by day
- Will negotiate if: heroes understand the situation and are sympathetic; she's not unreasonable
- Will fight if: heroes try to destroy her body, or threaten Lara, or attack without talking
- Breaks under: threat to Lara (her one vulnerability), evidence that the heroes will actually stop Teldi themselves, being confronted with the fact that her kills will never end — the curse demands feeding

**Toolkit — Lara Murano:**
- Appears: sturdy, protective, fierce
- Wants: Sialia safe, the shop intact, Teldi dead or gone
- Has: knowledge of Sialia's secret, a sharp tongue, no combat ability
- Will cooperate if: heroes seem sympathetic; will physically block them from the back room if threatened

**Lara Murano** — not a combatant

Protective, fierce, loves her wife, will not betray her

- Defense — Health: Health Threshold 3, Health 1
- Defense — Morale: Morale Threshold 4, Grit 2 (love and stubbornness), Morale 10
- Offense — Sway: +0; Fixed Damage 3 (righteous anger)
- Abilities: Malus 5
- Special Abilities: Bolster Morale (cost varies — can bolster Sialia's Morale)
- Refresh Tokens: 1

### If heroes arrive during the day

Sialia is human, working the shop. Lara is there too. The shop is open. This is a social scene. Sialia will be friendly and helpful unless the heroes reveal they know what she is. If confronted with evidence, she doesn't deny it — she explains. "They were monsters. They hurt people for years and nobody stopped them. I did."

This is the best path for a social resolution. The heroes can:
- Convince Sialia to stop killing (requires a credible plan to stop Teldi another way)
- Negotiate: she stops if they deliver Teldi to justice
- Let her finish the job (she only has Teldi and maybe Grazza left)
- Turn her in to the authorities (but which authorities? Teldi IS the Watch here)

### If heroes arrive at night

Sialia is out hunting. Only Lara is here, guarding an empty body lying in the soaking tub. This is more likely to become a combat encounter if the heroes try to destroy the body. Lara will fight (socially — she can't physically stop them) and plead. Sialia returns if her body is threatened — she can sense it.

**Safety valve — the unwinnable fight:** If heroes fight penanggalan-Sialia at night without knowing the "destroy the body" vulnerability, she regenerates 3 Health per round and they cannot win. Don't let this become a frustrating grind. After 2 rounds of futile combat, use one of these escape routes:
- **Lara screams** from inside: "You can't kill her like that! You have to destroy the body — it's in the shop!" (She'd rather reveal the secret than watch her wife torn apart.)
- **Sialia taunts** mid-fight: "You can hit me all night. It won't matter. I'll be whole again by dawn." (Spend 3 Malus for a Sway Maneuver — heroes take Morale damage or realize they need a new plan.)
- **Shift the clue type** (per Robin Laws): make the vulnerability "immediately apparent" if the players are stuck. A hero with ANY investigative ability might notice that the wounds keep closing, the body in the tub is glowing faintly, and there's an obvious connection.

### If heroes don't come here voluntarily

The climax happens elsewhere. Sialia attacks Grazza or Teldi at their location, and the heroes can intervene. Or Teldi shows up at Murano's with hired muscle to burn it down, and the heroes must choose sides.

### Stat Block — Sialia Murano (Penanggalan Form)

**Only relevant in penanggalan form (night, head detached)**

Determined, sorrowful, terrifying, righteous

- Defense — Health: Health Threshold 4, Armor 2 (supernatural resilience), Health 10 per Hero
- Defense — Morale: Morale Threshold 4, Grit 2 (conviction), Morale 5 per Hero
- Offense — Warfare: +2; Damage Modifier +2 (visceral tendrils and bite)
- Offense — Sway: +1; Damage Modifier +1 (abject supernatural horror)
- Abilities: Malus 18
- Special Abilities:
  - **Flight** (always active) — Sialia flies and can attack from above
  - **Regeneration** (always active) — Regains 3 Health per round unless her empty body is being destroyed
  - **Fluid** (cost 6) — Can squeeze through any opening large enough for a human head
  - **Monstrous Ability** (cost 3) — Prehensile organs can grab and constrict; use as Warfare Maneuver (Restrain)
- Misc: Alertness Modifier +2 (scents blood and guilt), Stealth Modifier +2
- Refresh Tokens: 5

**Vulnerability:** Sialia can only be permanently killed by destroying her empty human body while her head is separated from it. If the body is destroyed, the penanggalan form dissipates within minutes. If the body is intact, she regenerates fully by dawn.

**Sialia in human form** (day, or if she reattaches):

Kind, weary, resigned

- Defense — Health: Health Threshold 3, Armor 0, Health 8
- Defense — Morale: Morale Threshold 4, Grit 2 (conviction), Morale 10
- Offense — Warfare: +0; Damage Modifier +0 (kitchen knife)
- Offense — Sway: +1; Damage Modifier +2 (genuine moral authority)
- Abilities: Malus 8
- Refresh Tokens: 3

## Resolutions (not scripted — these are the likely emergent outcomes)

**Heroes kill Sialia:**
Teldi's racket continues. Harbor Approach keeps suffering. The heroes got paid. Was it worth it? Teldi might even frame them for the murders to cover his tracks.

**Heroes expose Teldi:**
Using the ledgers from Zarra's room or Belto's warehouse, heroes can turn Teldi over to the Watch hierarchy, the Church, a noble patron, or even the Triskadane (secret government). Teldi is arrested or flees. Sialia's targets are gone — does the curse stop feeding? (Up to the GM — probably not forever, but for now.)

**Heroes negotiate:**
Sialia stops if Teldi is dealt with. Heroes handle Teldi themselves. The pickle shop stays open. Harbor Approach is safer. Sialia's curse remains — a problem for another day.

**Heroes walk away:**
Sialia finishes the job. Teldi dies. Harbor Approach is free of the racket. A cursed penanggalan still lives on Cutter's Lane. Is that a problem?

**Heroes burn it all down:**
Kill Sialia AND expose Teldi. Maximum justice, maximum chaos. Harbor Approach needs a new Watch Commander and has lost its protector and its oppressor.

- Players will likely visit 2 of 3 middle nodes, not all 3. That's by design — the network has enough redundancy.

## GM Cheat Sheet: Running This Adventure

### Before the session
- Print: 5 character templates, 5 rules sheets, 2 sorcery cheat sheets, 3 filled pregens as fallbacks
- Bring: lots of d6s (players will only have what you bring), pencils, scrap paper
- Prepare: a bowl + tokens for Refresh (coins, pebbles, anything)
- Read: this document. Especially the toolkits. You don't need to memorize scenes — know what each NPC wants and what clues exist.

### At the table
- **Don't explain all the rules.** "You need 1d6. Target is 4. If you have the right ability, you get the clue automatically." That's the pitch. Explain combat during Scene 0. Explain spends when they first come up.
- **Give clues freely.** If a player has the right ability and does the right thing, they get the information. No rolls. This is the GUMSHOE promise — don't break it.
- **Adjust clue delivery based on pacing.** If players are stuck, make clues immediately apparent. If they're sharp, let them ask for things.
- **Reward Investigative spends visually.** When someone spends a point for bonus damage, describe the effect dramatically. "You know exactly where the gap in his armor is — your blade slides through and he screams."
- **NPCs want things.** Teldi wants survival. Sialia wants justice. Lara wants safety. Vess wants profit. Play their wants, not a script.
- **It's OK if the heroes do something unexpected.** You have the situation. You know what everyone wants. Improvise from that.

### Common first-timer mistakes to watch for
- Players hoarding Investigative points: remind them spending doesn't reduce their expertise
- Players rolling for Investigative abilities: gently stop them. "You have Leechcraft? You just know this."
- Players not spending on General ability tests: show them the math. "The target is 4. You roll a d6. If you spend 3 points, you auto-succeed."
- Players forgetting Refresh tokens: point at the bowl after every dropped mook

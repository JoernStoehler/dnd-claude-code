# Sin-drinker: An Adventure Seed for Swords of the Serpentine

**By Kevin Kulp**

## Adventure Overview

This adventure seed for *Swords of the Serpentine* fills one to two game sessions. The Heroes are hired by a corrupt Watch Commander to eliminate a mysterious monster, but discover the "monster" is actually a vigilante targeting criminals. The scenario emphasizes moral complexity over combat.

## Core Premise

"A corrupt commander of the City Watch is being stalked by a penanggalan, a monstrosity" that feeds on sin and depravity. The creature systematically kills the Commander's criminal associates before targeting him. Unwilling to expose his crimes through official channels, he hires outside mercenaries instead.

## Key NPCs

**Watch Commander Antonio Teldi** -- Secretly runs protection rackets in Harbor Approach. Apparently competent but cowardly and greedy. Uses Watch authority to shield illegal operations.

**Sialia Murano** -- A penanggalan cursed to separate her head and viscera nightly to hunt sin. By day, she co-owns Murano's Pickles with her wife Lara, soaking her organs in vinegar to fit back into her body. She's a vigilante rather than mindless monster.

**Lara Murano** -- Sialia's wife and business partner. Knows her spouse's secret and fiercely protective.

**Zarra** -- A mercenary enforcer collecting protection money for Teldi. Murdered early in the adventure.

**Belto** -- Criminal contractor selling illegal poisons. Connected to thieves' guilds. Also murdered.

## Adventure Structure

**Scene 1: The Offer** -- Teldi hires the Heroes, providing Badge of Office and 2-3 Wealth for eliminating the killer.

**Scene 2 (Optional): Enemy Interference** -- A brief encounter with one of the Heroes' enemies.

**Scene 3: Stabbing** -- Investigation of Zarra's locked-room murder. Multiple clues suggest supernatural strangulation, blood drainage, and vinegar scent.

**Scene 4: Montage** -- Travel encounter across Harbor Approach, including mention of distinctive pickles.

**Scene 5: Poisonous Fire** -- Investigation of Belto's warehouse murder, showing internal poisoning and intestinal tracks.

**Scene 6: The Cleaners** -- Thieves' guild members arrive to clean evidence. Can be negotiated with or fought.

**Scene 7: Epiphany** -- Heroes realize Teldi is corrupt and the killer hunts his associates.

**Scene 8: Murano's** -- Final confrontation at the pickle factory.

## Investigation Clues

From the stabbing scene: Zarra was drained of blood through punctures, impaled with her own weapons, and fought her attacker. Long red hairs found in her hands. Peculiar vinegar scent on weapons and window.

From the poisoning scene: Blood drainage without spillage, prehensile organ tracks on floors/ceilings, alchemical poison vials mixed internally, distinctive vinegar-salt-herb odor.

Knowledge checks reveal penanggalan folklore: nocturnal creatures appearing as fireflies, requiring vinegar to compress organs, vulnerable only when their empty torso is destroyed.

## Sialia Murano Stats

- **Health Threshold** 4, **Armor** 2; **Health** 10 per Hero
- **Morale Threshold** 4, **Grit** 2; **Morale** 5 per Hero
- **Warfare** +2 (**Damage** +2); **Sway** +1 (**Damage** +1)
- **Malus** 18
- **Abilities:** Fluid (cost 6), Monstrous Ability (cost 3), Flight, Regenerate (destroyed body prevents reformation)
- **Modifiers:** Alertness +2 (scents blood/sin), Stealth +2
- **Special:** Regenerates fully if headless body remains intact. Vulnerable only to destruction of empty torso while separated.

## Themes & Resolution

The adventure explores moral ambiguity. Sialia commits murders but targets genuinely corrupt individuals harming Harbor Approach. Players may expose Teldi, fake Sialia's death, eliminate her permanently, or negotiate alternative solutions.

The author notes: "This isn't a combat-heavy adventure; the drama is the heroes making difficult decisions when they realize the human who hired them is more monstrous than the actual monster."

Multiple endings exist based on player choices regarding exposure, mercy, and justice.

# Three Things About Eversink: Swords of the Serpentine's Core Setting

**By Kevin Kulp**

## Overview

This article introduces Eversink, the core setting for the swords and sorcery RPG *Swords of the Serpentine*. The game is designed for flexible settings but shines when centered in an urban environment comparable to *Thieves' World*'s Sanctuary or Lankhmar.

## The Three Core Setting Elements

### 1. The Buildings are Always Sinking

Buildings in Eversink descend approximately "8 centimeters a year" into the soil, causing most structures to lose their ground floor every fifty years. The reason remains unknown despite the city's thousand-year history. Some buildings sink faster or slower than average, and rare instances see buildings drop multiple stories in a single day. This creates an extensive undercity of buried structures that adventurers can explore, adding depth to urban exploration.

### 2. The City and the Goddess are One

Eversink itself comprises the physical body of Denari, the goddess of commerce and civilization. "Every coin exchanged is a prayer, and every transaction a sacrament." The goddess blesses trade within her borders but cannot extend her protection everywhere. Sorcerous corruption damages her, and she only knows what faithful followers communicate through prayer. Small gods and demons compete for worship by creating rival factions.

### 3. Statues are Everywhere, Because Statues Represent Souls

Burial underground is impossible due to flooding, so the culture developed funerary statues to ensure souls reach Denari's heaven. "It's illegal to destroy a funerary statue, because that could destroy a soul." Hundreds of thousands of statues fill the city -- in canals, on roofs, in homes, and wall niches. Crime frequently involves these sacred objects, creating unique plot opportunities.

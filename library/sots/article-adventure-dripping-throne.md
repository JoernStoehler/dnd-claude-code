# The Dripping Throne: Adventure Seed for Swords of the Serpentine

**Author:** Kevin Kulp

## Overview

This adventure seed presents a morally complex scenario where heroes investigate a merchant's ghost and uncover a fishing guild's dark secret. The adventure spans approximately one to two sessions.

## Core Premise

"The ghost of a murdered merchant asks the Heroes to investigate his murder. Doing so lands the Heroes in deep danger from a seemingly innocuous source, and presents them with a difficult decision: what happens when the thing that helps people isn't the thing that's both ethically and morally right?"

The Fishing Guild sacrifices one human monthly to ensure abundant catches feeding 100,000 citizens.

## Adventure Structure

### Scene 1: The Ghost

A murdered merchant's spirit hires the heroes, offering Wealth as payment. The ghost either appears directly to someone with Spirit Sight or contacts heroes through a beggar.

### Scene 2: The Cove (optional)

Heroes discover a pond containing approximately 800 corpses spanning 80 years, with monthly additions. They fight two waves of boneteeth (dog-sized hostile fish), roughly 2-4 per hero per wave.

### Scene 3: The Witness

Heroes locate an elderly beggar named Crow who identifies "Eel," a fisherman involved in the murder. Investigation reveals the Glorious Assemblage of Attentive Fisherfolk operates from Fish Hall.

### Scene 4: The Accusation

Confronting Eel typically triggers combat. Eel summons allies and calls Drunken Sailors and Brutes for support.

### Scene 5: Resolution

Multiple outcomes available: allowing the guild to continue, exposing them to authorities, or raiding Fish Hall themselves.

## Key Adversary Stats

### Eel (Fisherman)

- **Health Threshold** 4, **Health** 10 per Hero
- **Morale Threshold** 4, **Morale** 5 per Hero
- **Warfare** +1; **Damage** +3 (fish-gutting knife)
- **Special Abilities:** Allies (Fish Hall, cost 3), Strength, Summoning (Fisherfolk, cost 3)
- **Stealth Modifier** +1
- **Refresh Tokens:** 5

### Boneteeth

- **Health Threshold** 3, **Health** 1
- **Morale Threshold** 3, **Morale** 1
- **Warfare** +0; **Fixed Damage** 3
- Immune to language-based Morale attacks
- Anyone swimming takes 3 Morale damage/round from fear

## Plot Elements

The guild's guildmistress is **Julliana Fishhall**, a 40-year priestess of an ancient fish god demanding monthly sacrifice. Deep in Fish Hall's basements sits the "Dripping Throne" -- made from Eversink's original bridge cornerstone -- where victims' organs are ceremonially placed during new moons.

Heroes face genuine ethical complexity: the sacrifices genuinely enable the city's food security. The adventure explores whether one death monthly constitutes acceptable cost for widespread prosperity.

# Exploring Bookhounds of Eversink

**By Kevin Kulp**

## Overview

"Bookhounds of Eversink" is a quick-start campaign setup for *Swords of the Serpentine* where adventures center on acquiring rare, powerful books in a swords & sorcery setting. The article presents five distinct campaign frameworks with sample plot hooks for each.

## Five Campaign Setups

### 1. Antiquarian Booksellers

Characters work for a rare bookstore acquiring texts through legal or extralegal means. This setup provides episodic play with clear payouts and allows GMs to involve patrons from diverse backgrounds -- nobles, small gods, and various factions.

**Sample hooks include:** retrieving stolen historical tomes, procuring divine manifestos, and navigating anonymous patron schemes.

### 2. University Professors

Faculty and staff at Imperator University or a custom institution hunt dangerous magical books while navigating academic politics. The framework allows fish-out-of-water adventures as comfortable scholars face deadly quests.

**Sample adventures:** capturing sentient grimoires, countering magical hiveminds, and competing to locate elite textbooks.

### 3. Ancient Nobility

Characters serve noble families in competitive acquisitions of rare books for prestige rather than knowledge. This creates opportunities for masquerade intrigue, infiltration, and potential dual loyalties.

**Sample hooks:** managing politically explosive histories, blackmailing rival families, and recovering lost family libraries.

### 4. Church Inquisitors

Specialized teams retrieve heretical or dangerous texts for the Church of Denari's archives. The moral complexity of rooting out "heresy" while maintaining ethics creates compelling tension.

**Sample adventures:** handling heretical scrolls with dangerous truth-revealing properties, pursuing demon-summoning tomes, and disarming sorcerous traps.

### 5. Sorcerous Cabals

Members hunt grimoires, true names, and forbidden knowledge for mysterious superiors while opposing church authority and established power structures.

**Sample hooks:** competing in dangerous acquisitions, outsmarting the Inquisition, and completing complex theft assignments with hidden agendas.

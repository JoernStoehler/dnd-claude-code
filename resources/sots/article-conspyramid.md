# The Conspyramid

**Origin:** Night's Black Agents by Kenneth Hite (Pelgrane Press)
**References:**
- https://thealexandrian.net/wordpress/49659/roleplaying-games/review-nights-black-agents
- https://thealexandrian.net/wordpress/36575/roleplaying-games/thinking-about-urbancrawls-part-9-new-school-urbancrawls

## Core Concept

The Conspyramid is a campaign structure for organizing conspiracies and opposition networks. The conspiracy is broken down into **nodes** — sources of blood/funding/protection, cults, institutions, infrastructure, front companies — arranged into a pyramidal structure and connected to each other.

## Structure

Nodes are arranged in rows from bottom (street level) to top (core leadership):

- **Row 1 (bottom):** Street-level operatives, minor assets, front businesses
- **Row 2:** Mid-level management, regional operations
- **Row 3:** Senior leadership, key infrastructure
- **Row 4:** Inner circle
- **Row 5 (top):** The mastermind / core threat

## Mechanical Integration

The default difficulty of relevant tests equals **3 + the row number**:
- Row 1: Difficulty 4
- Row 2: Difficulty 5
- Row 3: Difficulty 6
- etc.

This naturally escalates tension as players work up the pyramid.

## Why It Works

The Conspyramid simultaneously shows:
1. How the conspiracy organization works (abstract structure)
2. How the PCs can investigate it (investigation paths)
3. Natural difficulty progression (higher rows = harder)

Players work their way up the Conspyramid to the core leadership at its pinnacle. At any point, they can choose which node to investigate next based on what clues they've found.

## Adaptation for SotS

The Conspyramid adapts directly to SotS's faction-based play:
- Each node maps to a faction member, location, or operation
- Investigative abilities provide the clues connecting nodes
- Allegiance points interact with faction nodes
- The difficulty scaling maps to SotS's ability pool management

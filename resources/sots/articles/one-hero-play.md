# One-Hero Play: Two-Player Adventures in Swords of the Serpentine

**By Kevin Kulp**

## Overview

*Swords of the Serpentine* supports single-player gameplay without requiring the One-2-One system mechanics. This design echoes classic fantasy literature featuring solitary heroes like Conan or Elric undertaking adventures independently or with minimal companions.

## Character Creation Adjustments

Solo players receive 14 Investigative Build points instead of the standard 10 available in five-person groups. An additional bonus point becomes available when choosing a single profession, though diversification often proves more practical for one-hero play by expanding available options during investigation.

Per the core rulebook (p. 269), solo heroes also gain an additional Ally point.

## Example Character: Captain Fayne Chaskin

A deposed pirate queen inspired by historical figure Ching Shih, Captain Chask fled to Eversink to rebuild her strength.

**Drives:** Wielding deadly force; pursuing independent courses of action; establishing authority

**Combat Stats:**
- Health: Threshold 3, Armor 1, Total 8
- Morale: Threshold 4, Grit 1, Total 10
- Sway: 5 (commanding modifier +1)
- Warfare: 8 (rapier modifier +1)

**Solo Build Investigative Abilities:** Command 3, Intimidation 1, Liar's Tell 1, Nobility 1, Servility 1, Ridiculous Luck 1, Scurrilous Rumors 1, Skullduggery 4

**Allegiances:** Ally: Ancient Nobility 1, Outlanders 4; Enemy: Mercanti 1

## Player Guidance

**Creative Problem-Solving:** When lacking specific abilities, employ alternative skills creatively or recruit NPCs possessing needed expertise. Sidekicks effectively fill capability gaps.

**Combat Strategy Against Mooks:**
- Utilize Taunt to challenge leaders to single combat, bypassing regular enemies
- Spend Intimidation points to negotiate rather than fight
- Use Flashback with Charm to establish prior relationships

For overwhelming numbers, activate Warfare, Sway, or Sorcery at 8+ ranks in a single devastating attack to eliminate multiple mooks efficiently.

**Resource Management:** Deploy Ally points liberally for backup in any situation lacking convenient support, whether healing, expertise, or tactical assistance.

## GM Guidance

**Adventure Selection:** Avoid scenarios poorly suited to your hero's strengths. Warfare-focused characters facing Morale-only opponents create frustration. Prioritize adventures leveraging the hero's abilities.

**Sidekick Benefits:** Include a capable companion providing missing Investigative abilities, emergency healing, and engaging roleplay opportunities.

**Combat Scaling:** Handwave or eliminate excessive mooks. Solo heroes naturally focus on dramatically significant targets rather than speedbumps.

**Investigation Support:** Summarize discovered clues, interviewed contacts, and visited locations to prevent accidental dead ends, since players lack peers to brainstorm with.

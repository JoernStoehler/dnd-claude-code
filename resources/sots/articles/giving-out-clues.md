# Give a Clue: Giving Out Clues in GUMSHOE
**Author:** Robin D. Laws

## Core Concept

GUMSHOE ensures players gather essential mystery clues through investigative abilities and described actions, rather than random chance. The system remains fluid because GMs have freedom in delivery methods.

## Two Primary Clue Categories

### Immediately Apparent
Clues provided without requiring player action — simply by entering a scene or possessing the right ability. The GM announces these to the appropriate investigator. Used when believability demands it (an expert would obviously notice something) or when playability requires it (players might otherwise miss essential information).

### Action-Dependent
Clues requiring players to specify actions before revelation. Actions range from basic ("searching the room") to specific ("performing a centrifuge test"). The player describes what they're doing, then the GM provides the clue if their character has the appropriate ability.

## Shifting Clue Types

GMs should adjust clue presentation based on session dynamics:

- Convert action-dependent to immediately apparent when players struggle to progress
- Provide hints rather than direct clues to guide proactive players
- Reward player initiative by converting immediately apparent clues into action-dependent ones
- Adjust effort required based on pacing, group concentration, and player knowledge

The underlying principle: "The degree of effort players must go through to accumulate clues is a matter for constant and sensitive adjustment, based on factors including session pacing."

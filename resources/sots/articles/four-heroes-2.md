# Four Heroes #2: Swords of the Serpentine Pre-made Heroes

**Author:** Kevin Kulp

## Overview

This article presents four pre-generated player characters for *Swords of the Serpentine*, demonstrating character creation flexibility across different classes. The heroes emphasize political power through high Allegiance allocations rather than traditional sword & sorcery archetypes, all rooted in the city of Eversink.

## Featured Characters

### Sentinel: Exalted Arbiter Tebriel

Head of the Church of Denari, this pompous cleric was banished by his goddess to learn humility. Key mechanics include Health 8, Morale 10, and Sway 10 with a damage modifier. Notable abilities include Command, Laws & Traditions, and Preparedness (with flashback capability). His 5-rank Ally status in the Church of Denari provides significant institutional power while remaining hidden from the organization.

### Sorcerer: Ctol-Cwogohatl (Catol)

An ancient small god of tides trapped in human form, blending Sorcerer, Sentinel, and Warrior investigative abilities. Stats include Health 6, Morale 12, Sorcery 10, and Sway 5. Corruption spheres cover Blood, Earth, and Water. This character demonstrates how game mechanics accommodate unconventional concepts without requiring rules modifications.

### Thief: Mother Silgada

Grandmother of Thieves controlling Eversink's criminal underworld. Health 6, Morale 12, Sway 11 with persuasive modifier. Her 5-rank Thieves' Guild allegiance enables orchestrating crimes city-wide while maintaining plausible deniability through figureheads.

### Warrior: Hessia

Mercenary liaison to the City Defense Committee with Health 10, Morale 8, Warfare 8, and Sway 8. Equipped with the ceremonial blade "Bargainer," her allegiances to mercenary companies and Triskadane government provide military leverage without requiring combat-heavy gameplay.

## Design Philosophy

The article emphasizes that Allegiances serve as political chess pieces, establishing characters' societal roles and power bases independent of combat mechanics.

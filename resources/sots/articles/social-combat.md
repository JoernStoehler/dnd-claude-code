# Social Combat in Swords of the Serpentine
**Author:** Kevin Kulp

## Core Mechanics

### Morale
The game replaces traditional defense with Morale, measuring a character's willpower and confidence. When Morale drops below zero and continues toward -12, characters become Unstable, then Panicking, finally Broken. Defeating enemies this way avoids messy consequences like murder trials.

### Sway
Sway functions as the social combat ability, equivalent to Warfare for physical attacks. Characters describe their approach — witty banter, intimidation, or threats — and if successful, inflict Morale damage. Critical hits deal extra damage dice.

### Social Investigative Abilities
After landing a Sway attack, players spend Investigative ability points to deal additional Morale damage. For example, spending Taunt points makes opponents "incoherently furious." These points refresh each adventure.

### Maneuvers
When spending points feels excessive, Morale-based Maneuvers let players manipulate NPCs through persuasion or deception. Targets roll d6 to resist; they can spend Morale points to add to rolls, essentially damaging themselves to resist suggestions.

### Teamwork
Players assign their Sway damage to allies' attacks, ensuring no one gets sidelined when specialists dominate combat.

## Design Philosophy

The system draws from classic fantasy traditions featuring social conflict — from Tolkien's dragon-talk to Terry Pratchett's wit-based problem-solving.

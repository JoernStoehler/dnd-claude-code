# Strange Bedfellows: Manipulating Politics in Swords of the Serpentine

**By Kevin Kulp**

## Overview

This article explains how *Swords of the Serpentine* enables characters to meaningfully "change the world around you" through political faction manipulation mechanics.

## Political Factions in Fantasy

The article opens by noting that fantasy literature relies heavily on political factions as "movers and shakers who drive plot." Examples include Glen Cook's *Black Company*, Scott Lynch's *Lies of Locke Lamora*, and Terry Pratchett's *Discworld* series. Heroes interact with these factions as allies, neutrals, or enemies.

## Allegiances System

**Core Mechanic:** Political factions function as Investigative abilities in SotS. New heroes receive two ranks in Allegiances, distributed as either both in one faction (building reputation) or split between two different allies.

**Information Gathering:** Players spend Allegiance ranks to access faction knowledge. A thief with Allegiance ranks in "Outlanders" and "Sorcerous Cabals" can uncover information about foreign influence or secret cults during adventures.

**Pool Point Manipulation:** Beyond gathering clues, players spend pool points to call favors and manipulate political situations. The example provided shows using Sorcerous Cabals influence to cause a false accusation against a target, creating tactical advantage without requiring truth.

## Enemies System

Heroes also begin with one rank of Enemies. Unlike Allegiances, the GM (not the player) controls when Enemy pool points activate, creating complications and obstacles. This represents rival adventurers, hostile factions, or unreliable informants.

## Temporary Favor and Enmity

Adventures generate temporary Allegiance and Enemy pool points representing newly acquired favor or hatred. These temporary pools lack investigative function but provide flexible currency for faction manipulation across multiple scenes.

## Customization Framework

The article encourages GMs to create 8-12 custom factions matched to their campaign's scope -- from neighborhood conflicts to international adventures. The system scales based on where heroes maintain influence.

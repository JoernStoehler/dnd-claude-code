# Don't Prep Plots

**Source:** https://thealexandrian.net/wordpress/4147/roleplaying-games/dont-prep-plots
**Author:** Justin Alexander

## Core Principle

Don't prep plots, prep situations.

A **plot** is a predetermined sequence of events (A → B → C). A **situation** is a set of circumstances where outcomes depend on player actions.

## Why Situations Work Better

The plot-based approach creates a "chain of potential failure points." Each step depends on specific player choices, forcing GMs to railroad when players deviate.

Situation-based prep focuses on:
- Designing locations
- Statting NPCs and enemies
- Establishing NPC goals
- Creating a "toolkit" of resources

## Essential Principles

- **Three Clue Rule:** Include at least three methods for PCs to discover crucial information
- **Goal-Oriented Opponents:** Define what antagonists are trying to accomplish, not contingencies
- **Know Your Toolkit:** Prep personnel, equipment, locations, and information as flexible resources
- **Avoid Contingency Planning:** Don't script "if players do X, then bad guys do Y"

## Practical Benefits

- Significantly less prep work
- Player choices become meaningful
- Improvisation becomes manageable
- Adapts naturally to unexpected directions
- Empowers players rather than constraining them

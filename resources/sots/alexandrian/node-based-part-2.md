# Node-Based Scenario Design – Part 2: Choose Your Own Adventure

## The Core Problem

Many game masters follow this logic: (1) Players need meaningful choices, (2) Adventures require structure, (3) Therefore, prepare for each possible choice. This leads to exponentially expanding branching paths.

Alexander illustrates this with diagrams showing how two choices at each decision point create eight steps of material for three actual play steps. He notes: "You're preparing 5 times as much material to supply the same amount of playing time."

## Why This Fails

Even attempts to collapse branches together still require designing content that will never be used. More fundamentally, players will always discover unforeseen options, forcing game masters back into artificially limiting choices or scrambling improvisation.

The approach becomes self-defeating: despite extensive preparation, unpredictable player behavior undermines the entire system.

## The Solution Teased

Alexander concludes this dead-end approach and points toward node-based scenario design as the answer, specifically through "the inversion of the Three Clue Rule."

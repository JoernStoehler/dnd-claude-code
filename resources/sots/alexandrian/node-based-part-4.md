# Node-Based Scenario Design – Part 4: Sample Scenario

## Overview

This article presents a practical example of node-based scenario design using a Counter-Terrorism Unit (CTU) Las Vegas setting. The scenario begins when agents receive intelligence about payments made on a storage unit linked to terrorist operations.

## The Starting Node: North Las Vegas Self-Storage

The initial location contains a storage space filled with empty cardboard boxes concealing a recent removal of items. Three primary clues can be discovered here:

**Clue 1**: Rental records identify Yassif Mansoor as the lessee, with a fake address. Database searches reveal his actual apartment at Broadstone Indigo on Azure Avenue (Node A).

**Clue 2**: A bellboy uniform from the Bellagio hotel and casino is present (Node B).

**Clue 3**: A disposable cell phone with call logs tracing to Officer Frank Nasser of Las Vegas police (Node C).

An ancillary clue includes detonation caps found behind the door track.

## Node A: Yassif Mansoor's Apartment

The apartment contains eight terrorists in various locations. Key discoveries include:

**Clue 1**: Charred documentary evidence in a storage closet trash can reveals Radio Shack shipping manifests. Tracking the order provides bomb-building materials and a credit card number, which shows recent activity at the Bellagio.

**Clue 2**: Interrogating captured terrorists reveals Mansoor met with Officer Nasser at the apartment.

An ancillary discovery: six suicide-bomb vests are stored in a walk-in closet, intended as a second wave of attacks.

## Node B: The Bellagio Hotel

Players can locate Mansoor's rented suite through credit card tracking or other investigative methods. The suite contains Mansoor and six armed terrorists guarding a large bomb smuggled in via bellboy uniforms and luggage trolleys.

**Clue 1**: Identifying Mansoor enables tracking his home address.

**Clue 2**: Microfilm sewn into Mansoor's jacket contains records proving Officer Nasser embezzled from an undercover drug buy fund—evidence used for blackmail.

## Node C: Officer Frank Nasser

The compromised officer has assisted the terrorists through various means, including providing C4 from police lockup and intimidating officers to drop traffic citations.

**Clue 1**: Nasser is more likely to cooperate under questioning than Mansoor, particularly when confronted with evidence of his crimes.

**Clue 2**: Surveillance of Nasser reveals visits to both the Bellagio and Mansoor's apartment before planned attacks.

## Design Philosophy

This structure demonstrates how multiple clues at each location create redundancy in navigation. Players need discover only one clue per node to progress, though finding additional clues provides corroborating evidence and enhanced understanding. The design prepares situations rather than rigid plots, allowing flexible player interaction and multiple resolution approaches.

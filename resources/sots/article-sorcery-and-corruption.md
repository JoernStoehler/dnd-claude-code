# The Cost of Corruption: Sorcery in Swords of the Serpentine

**By Kevin Kulp**

## Overview

This article explains the sorcery system in *Swords of the Serpentine*, a GUMSHOE RPG emphasizing dark, dangerous magic that "corrupts and has a cost."

## Core Mechanics

**Two Key Abilities:**

- **Sorcery** (General ability): Used for attacks and damage dealing. Rank 8+ affects multiple foes.
- **Corruption** (Investigative ability): Measures magical potential; higher ranks enable world-altering effects. Spent points refresh only at adventure's end.

Sorcerers spend Sorcery points in combat and can expend Corruption points for powerful effects that defy normal rules -- stopping time, melting walls, or flying -- provided effects align with their chosen Spheres.

## Narrative Origins

Sorcerous power stems from two sources:

1. Ancient Serpentine folk writings that possess the caster's mind
2. Pacts with small gods or demons residing within the sorcerer's soul

Both grant power at a cost, creating moral complexity and internal conflict.

## Spheres and Description

Players define Sorcerous Spheres -- thematic frameworks governing how magic appears. A Plant sphere sorcerer describes attacks through vegetation; a Rat/Smoke sorcerer uses vermin and black vapors. "Rules dictate damage; you describe however you want."

## Corruption Cost

Powerful spells pollute surrounding areas or corrupt the caster's appearance. Players choose whether effects harm allies' morale or alter their own physical form -- explaining why fantasy sorcerers often appear unnaturally changed.

## Variant Option

**Witchery** offers access to alchemy, poison, disease, and mesmerism spheres without corruption risks.

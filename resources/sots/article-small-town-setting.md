# A Small-Town Setting for Swords of the Serpentine

**By Kevin Kulp**

## Player Pitch: The Town of Joining

Joining is a compact settlement nestled within the Cathedral Woods, a vast forest with ancient trees comparable to California's giant sequoias. Home to fewer than a thousand residents, the community sustains itself through hunting, fishing, foraging, and trade with distant caravan leaders who exchange goods for rare local plants and herbal compounds.

The setting presents an intriguing contradiction: while Joining appears peaceful, several mysteries linger. "Ancient and feral forest gods; druids who lair within the forest; wardens who patrol the area" suggest hidden dangers. Crumbling ruins hint at a forgotten prosperous past, and family heirlooms suggest former wealth with no clear explanation for its disappearance.

## Interesting Features

Players help define Joining by specifying additional distinctive elements. Examples include:

- Trees serve as street names rather than conventional addresses
- Buildings occupy both ground level and elevated tree platforms, with prestigious structures positioned highest
- Ghost-lights flicker between trees, allegedly revealing distant locations
- "Door Day" occurs monthly during new moons, when admirers leave anonymous gifts
- Summer festivals celebrate games, feasts, and apprentice selection
- The Grove of Arches provides a serene, sacred forest sanctuary
- A mysterious Inn generates ongoing speculation despite its modest four-room capacity
- A century-long feud between the Tavish family (hunters/guards) and Daunton family (foragers/old gods practitioners) repeatedly erupts in violence
- Shapeshifters allegedly hunt humans within the woods
- Extensive ruins cover the landscape; particularly large foundations upstream carry taboo significance

## Available Allegiances

Eight primary factions structure relationships:

- **Town government**: The pragmatic, safety-focused mayor controls town infrastructure
- **Townsfolk**: Social standing determines community acceptance
- **Sheriff and deputy**: Law enforcement figures offering trust or suspicion
- **Wardens**: Mysterious forest guardians with unknown agendas
- **Outsiders**: Traveling merchants, witches, and traders spreading reputation
- **Church of the new gods**: A charismatic Tavish minister leads the congregation
- **Tavish family**: Former mercenaries dominating professional hunting
- **Daunton family**: Forest foragers championing ancient spiritual traditions

## GM Pitch: Hidden History

(Warning: Contains spoilers)

Three centuries ago, Old Joining functioned as an interdimensional nexus. "Weekly portals in the nearby trees would open up to a specific different site in both this world and others," enabling travelers and traders across roughly fifty locations spanning six dimensions. The Inn of Arches provided lodging between dimensional transitions, while elite wardens and druids managed creatures emerging from gateways.

An unknown catastrophe -- potentially caused by either the Tavish or Daunton families -- terminated the portals and displaced the entire metropolitan population into another realm (faerie, hell, tropical island, or elsewhere remains up to the Game Master). The disaster remains largely forgotten, buried under religious extremism following the event.

## Campaign Structure

**Series One** focuses on heroes uncovering Joining's mysterious past through investigation of ancient sites, interactions with wardens, and gradual revelation of the nexus's fate. The series potentially concludes with restoring the gateway system.

**Series Two** escalates complexity as dimensional gates reactivate, attracting external factions and internal political upheaval. Characters may journey through portals, discovering Old Joining's transformed state and learning methods for resurrection.

**Future series** might explore Joining's transformation into a crucial multidimensional commerce hub, facing invasions through gateways, managing cross-cultural integration, or transitioning toward entirely different settings via dimensional portals.

# Serpentine Plot Hooks: Rebellious Factions Part 2

**By Kevin Kulp**

## Overview

This article provides plot hooks centered on rebel characters from the twelve factions in *Swords of the Serpentine*. Kulp emphasizes that "combining focused ambition with poor judgment is a great basis for an adventure," offering GMs quick-start material for last-minute games.

## Faction Plot Hooks

### Mercenaries

**Concept:** A rogue mercenary company funded by rival city Capria seeks to destabilize Eversink while maintaining legal protection.

**Key NPC:** Commander Lielle presents herself as trustworthy despite involvement in murders, mutiny, and terrorism. Her company charges reduced rates for jobs advancing their destabilization agenda, always ensuring clients bear legal liability.

**Plot Hook:** The Company of the Golden Coin repeatedly opposes the Heroes, leveraging pro-mercenary laws. They become personal antagonists, volunteering service to the Heroes' enemies.

### Monstrosities

**Concept:** Civic leaders make unusually generous decisions before disappearing -- victims of Ilgazar, a facestealer with reformist ideology.

**Key NPC:** Ilgazar theorizes that helping Eversink's poor thrive will benefit monstrosities feeding on healthier emotions. He systematically targets officials with authority to improve lower-class conditions.

**Plot Hook:** Investigating missing church and political leaders leads to discovering the actual victims while confronting whether to stop or support Ilgazar's goals.

### Outlanders

**Concept:** Princess Kayleth arrives seeking help reclaiming her stolen kingdom, attracting both allies and assassins.

**Key NPC:** Raised as a warrior, Kayleth needs resources and military support. Her backstory -- legitimacy, allies, secrets -- remains customizable for individual campaigns.

**Plot Hook:** Multiple factions compete to hire or eliminate the Princess, creating extended adventures involving mercenary companies and political intrigue.

### Sorcerous Cabals

**Concept:** The Valiant Order operates openly as a prestigious foreign religious group while secretly recruiting charismatic individuals as future political leaders sympathetic to demon worship.

**Key NPC:** Valiant Order members wield sorcery but carefully avoid externalizing corruption. Their goal involves creating natural leaders who'll eventually overthrow the Triskadane.

**Plot Hook:** The Inquisition suspects cult activity but needs proof. Heroes must infiltrate the Order, discovering dangers worse than anticipated, forcing difficult moral choices.

### Thieves' Guilds

**Concept:** The Guild of Feathers, facing extinction, finances talented thieves to rob rival guilds and frame the Guild of Goose-keepers for the crimes.

**Key NPC:** Guildmaster Orlando di Penna orchestrates the scheme through his sister-in-law and exceptional con artists. The plan aims to trigger disproportionate retaliation destroying the Goose-keepers.

**Plot Hook:** Heroes can approach from multiple angles -- as thieves, investigators, or faction agents -- while managing stolen goods and factional warfare consequences.

### Triskadane

**Concept:** A sorcerous cabal announces payment for a Triskadane coin during a heat wave, triggering city-wide chaos over divinely-selected council member positions.

**Key NPC:** The Children of Ytt deliberately create confusion, possibly masking more dangerous objectives beneath the surface chaos.

**Plot Hook:** Allied factions recruit Heroes for protection, artifact retrieval, or investigation, while the true cult agenda remains hidden.
